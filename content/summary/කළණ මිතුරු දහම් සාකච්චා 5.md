- Directly quoted / referred to, unattributed: *tajjo (sajjo) samannāhāro* ("the corresponding bringing-together / born of itself right there"); the Madhupiṇḍika proliferation sequence *yaṁ vedeti taṁ sañjānāti, yaṁ sañjānāti taṁ vitakketi, yaṁ vitakketi taṁ papañceti* ("what one feels one perceives, what one perceives one thinks of, what one thinks of one proliferates"); and *(sattaṁ) sattaṁ paripassati* ("one sees each just [as arisen there]").
- Terms used: *saññā* (perception), *phassa* (contact), *papañca* (proliferation), *māna* (conceit), and the standard aggregate-analysis pairs — inner/outer (*ajjhatta-bahiddhā*), near/far, self/other, subtle/gross (*oḷārika-sukhuma*), inferior/superior (*hīna-paṇīta*), past/present/future. - **Note on the source:** a short two-speaker session; the transcript ends mid-word ("the knot").

## Main dhamma points
- A short two-speaker "good-friend" dialogue showing that "sound" is only a recognition with nothing inside it, born right where it is recognized, so that all the aggregate-measures collapse and no cause — and no kamma — can be found.
- **"Sound" is only a recognition.** We do not even notice; we knot directly, taking "the sound is the bird's," never realizing that "sound" is only a recognition with no thing in it. "A dog is present" is held through "the dog's sound" — but no dog was present when the sound was recognized; the sound is born right there, recognized on the spot, not come from anywhere.
- **The measures collapse.** Seeing this, no "inner / outer" story forms, for it arose only at this moment; nor "near / far," nor "self / other," nor "subtle / gross," nor "inferior / superior" — we do not really measure "this is a loud sound, this a sweet one, this a harsh one," and where it never arose, whence such measures? So the standard aggregate-pairs do not apply, and "this sound existed in the past, exists now, could exist in future" cannot be thought — so conceit and agitation do not operate, and seeing "this, right here," the mouth locks up, the way to those pairs blocked.
- **Perception, thought, proliferation.** Seeing each just as arisen right there, one finds the "sound" consciousness has no description of what it is. Then *yaṁ vedeti taṁ sañjānāti* — what is felt is recognized as "a sound"; *yaṁ sañjānāti taṁ vitakketi* — the recognized sound is thought about, "whose is it, a bird's, a dog's?"; *yaṁ vitakketi taṁ papañceti* — thinking, proliferation forms, "that is a dog, or if a bird cried, a crow." The proliferation is then confirmed by all five senses — the built proliferation seen by eye, heard by ear, tasted — and so made real that it can no longer be falsified.
- **The knot tightens if forced.** Untying a knot loosens it, but pulling tightens it: hearing a sound and calling it false, saying "it did not arise," cannot be done and only tightens the knot. So what is shown is the *place where the knot does not fall* — where no knot falls there is nothing to untie, and that is the loosening, a place never stated before.
- **Born right there; no cause, no kamma.** *Tajjo samannāhāro* means arising from that very place, not come from anywhere; from this one "sound" everything can be distributed and seen, "what you mean is right there." The "sound" mind seems to come from somewhere, but the mind has no things in it, and sound and hearing all arise from that very place with no cause found anywhere — so nothing being obtained, one does not knot kamma to run saṁsāra or to suffer its results. "A crow's sound was heard" builds no proliferation once one sees, within the hearing, that no sound is obtained.
- **Show the knot, do not merely deny.** As before, "hearing cannot be pointed to"; merely sitting and declaring "there is no hearing, hearing is not found" does not work — one must exhibit the *knot*, showing what is actually happening here rather than what we assume.

## Summary

This short two-speaker good-friend dialogue turns on the recognition "sound." We do not even notice, the teacher says; we knot directly, taking "the sound is the bird's," and never realize that "sound" is only a recognition with nothing inside it — so that "a dog is present" is held through "the dog's sound," though no dog was present when the sound was recognized, the sound being born right there and not come from anywhere. Seeing this, no "inner / outer" story forms, for it arose only at this moment, and neither do "near / far," "self / other," "subtle / gross," or "inferior / superior," since we do not truly measure a sound as loud or sweet or harsh, and where nothing arose there are no measures; even "this sound existed before and will exist again" cannot be thought, so conceit and agitation do not operate, and the mouth simply locks up, the way to those pairs blocked. Seeing each just as arisen on the spot, one finds the "sound" consciousness has no description of what it is; and then the Madhupiṇḍika sequence unfolds — what is felt is recognized as "a sound," the recognized sound is thought about ("whose is it?"), and thinking proliferates into "a dog" or "a crow," a proliferation the five senses then confirm and make so real it can no longer be falsified.

The teacher stresses that the knot tightens when forced: to hear a sound and call it false, insisting "it did not arise," cannot be done and only pulls the knot tighter, so what he shows instead is the *place where the knot does not fall*, where nothing remains to untie — a loosening never stated before. This is *tajjo samannāhāro*, arising from that very place and not come from anywhere, so that from this one "sound" everything can be seen distributed around it; the "sound" mind seems to come from somewhere, but holds no things, and sound and hearing alike arise on the spot with no cause found anywhere — and nothing being obtained, one no longer knots kamma to run saṁsāra or to suffer its fruits, "a crow's sound was heard" building no proliferation once one sees, within the hearing, that no sound is obtained. He closes, as before, by insisting that one must not merely declare "there is no hearing" but must *exhibit the knot* itself, showing what is actually happening here rather than what we assume — the transcript breaking off on that very word, "the knot."
