## Main dhamma points
- A two-speaker "good-friend" dialogue on why the view cannot be understood by the one who has it — because the very seeker is a re-creation of the distortion — using the chair-and-shadow image to show that "I" and the seen world are two facets of one making that consciousness can never catch.
- **The seeker is a replica of the distortion.** The ordinary mind cannot see that this world and the "I" who meets it are two facets of one distortion, so it asks "why can I not understand this? how did I get caught in this foolishness?" — but the one searching, taking "I exist" as 100% real, never sees any flaw in the searcher; the very concept "how did *I* get caught" is itself the not-knowing of the true nature, a searching by an "I" for a truth about its own deception, and so is a portrait of the very foolishness it hunts.
- **Only a Buddha shows it; other teachers doubted the world, not the self.** Because ignorance is not cognizable to the ordinary mind, only a Buddha reveals it, which is why it is called *pubbe ananussutesu*, never heard before; all other teachers sought a solution "for me," each certain of himself to 200%, doubting only the world's making and never the seeker's own defect — no teacher ever saw that the seeker is a re-fabrication of the distortion, that the very centre from which he searches is itself built by this distortion.
- **Thinking cannot catch the flaw of thinking.** The flaw here cannot be found by thinking, for whatever we think seems right — as looking at a matchbox and matchstick to find the "fire" (which is in neither) yields only "there is no fire in these," so one slides unnoticed into the "there is not" extreme, never seeing that the obtained fruit has fallen to a "no-fruit." This is why one gets caught on the cause-and-fruit knot even after trying to escape: since the law of cause-and-fruit itself arises conditioned by ignorance, a consciousness born of fabrication can never catch the fabrication's flaw, and every attempt to escape rebuilds the same cause-and-fruit — *imasmiṁ sati idaṁ hoti* — trapping one again in "there-is / there-is-not."
- **Consciousness is a product of the fabrication it examines.** The fabrication is the five clinging-aggregates arising from form-feeling-perception-formations, and consciousness settles onto exactly that — *avijjā-paccayā saṅkhārā, saṅkhāra-paccayā viññāṇaṁ* — so a consciousness sprung from the fabrication can never grasp the fabrication's flaw; not seeing feeling as feeling and perception as perception, the two knot, and by the perception-marks (shape, colour) a "chair" is recognized, "seeing a chair," knotted with feeling into the permanent-pleasant-self we have clung to from birth, in which no flaw is ever felt — the system only ever flowing downstream, never against the stream, that being the very nature of ignorance.
- **The second thought is another thief in white cloth.** When a next thought says "the first thought was wrong," that too is a fresh consciousness holding the lie: the first consciousness said "a chair is seen," the second says "no chair can be met — only feeling, perception, not colour or shape," and one thinks one has escaped "chair"; but the very consciousness that shows the first as wrong is itself born grasping the lie, a thief now wearing a white cloth, and it is never seen that *this* correcting is also self-view — which is exactly why one thinks "I have understood the truth." What is built is persistence itself, not an escape.
- **The chair-and-shadow: "I" is the shadow of the view.** As a chair in sunlight casts a shadow that tilts and falls exactly as the chair is placed, inseparable from it yet not the chair, so consciousness casts the view: however the mind thinks, the view arises just so, and the "I" is the shadow of that thinking. When a consciousness shows the first thought false, "I" arises with it as its shadow — so it seems "I stand apart and I have realized the first was false," whereas the correcting consciousness and the "I" are shadow and object, and one wrongly takes it as "a flaw of consciousness caught me."
- **Seeing is only seeing; no one is caught.** "Seeing is only seeing" is no easy or memorizable place: rightly seen, "I" is born through the very seeing, so there is no "I" standing apart to analyze a seeing it met — and then who understands it *as* "seeing"? No separate one arises; even "it is seen" cannot be said, for there is no one to say it, the "one" being itself a creation of the seeing. So the question "how did *I* get caught in this?" dissolves: no one got caught, for the "one" is only a reflection within the seeing. As the shadow's mind holds no idea of "chair" and within "chair" no idea of "shadow," yet we knot "the chair's shadow" — neither identical nor separable — this is the very fabrication we do not know; and one must see the root of the making, not argue about the made things, for both "chair is the shadow" and "shadow exists apart from chair" are wrong, and any account given from consciousness stays trapped in those two.

## Summary

This two-speaker good-friend dialogue explains why the view cannot be understood by the one who holds it: the seeker is a re-creation of the very distortion. The ordinary mind cannot see that the world and the "I" who meets it are two facets of one distortion, so it asks "why can I not understand this, how did I get caught in this foolishness?" — yet taking "I exist" as wholly real, it never sees a flaw in the searcher, and the concept "how did *I* get caught" is itself the not-knowing of the true nature, a portrait of the foolishness it hunts. Because ignorance is not cognizable to the ordinary mind, only a Buddha reveals it — hence "never heard before" — while all other teachers sought a solution "for me," certain of themselves and doubting only the world, never seeing the seeker as a re-fabrication whose very centre is built by the distortion. The flaw cannot be caught by thinking, for whatever we think seems right: as looking at a matchbox and matchstick for "fire" yields only "there is no fire in these," one slides into the "there is not" extreme without noticing the fruit has fallen to a no-fruit. This is why one is re-caught on the cause-and-fruit knot even while trying to escape, since the law of cause-and-fruit itself arises conditioned by ignorance — *avijjā-paccayā saṅkhārā, saṅkhāra-paccayā viññāṇaṁ* — so a consciousness sprung from the fabrication can never grasp its flaw.

Not seeing feeling as feeling and perception as perception, the teacher shows, the two knot, and by the perception-marks a "chair" is recognized and knotted with feeling into the permanent-pleasant-self clung to from birth, in which no flaw is ever felt, the system only flowing downstream. When a next thought says "the first was wrong," that is another consciousness holding the lie — the first saying "a chair is seen," the second "no chair can be met, only feeling and perception" — a thief now in a white cloth, never seen to be self-view itself, which is why one thinks "I have understood." The chair-and-shadow image clinches it: as a chair casts a shadow that moves exactly as it is placed, inseparable yet not the chair, consciousness casts the view, and the "I" is the shadow of the thinking; when a consciousness shows the first thought false, "I" arises as its shadow, so it seems "I stand apart and have realized it," though the correcting consciousness and the "I" are shadow and object. Hence "seeing is only seeing" is no easy place: "I" is born through the very seeing, so no "I" stands apart to analyze it, and then who understands it as "seeing"? No separate one arises, and even "it is seen" cannot be said. So "how did *I* get caught?" dissolves — no one got caught, the "one" being only a reflection within the seeing — and as the shadow holds no idea of "chair" and "chair" no idea of "shadow," yet we knot "the chair's shadow," neither identical nor separable, this is the fabrication we do not know; one must see the root of the making, not the made things, since both "chair is the shadow" and "shadow exists apart" are wrong, and any account from consciousness stays trapped between them.
