## Sutta referred to
- **Kalaha-vivāda Sutta** (Sn 4.11) — named; the line *na saññasaññī na visaññasaññī, no pi asaññī na vibhūtasaññī* ("not percipient with perception, not with distorted perception, not non-percipient, not with perception abolished") is quoted and analysed clause by clause, as describing the arahant's perception (not perceiving as we do, not distorting, not perception-less, not perception-annihilated).
- Directly quoted / referred to, unattributed by title: *kammaṁ khettaṁ, viññāṇaṁ bījaṁ, taṇhā sineho* ("kamma the field, consciousness the seed, craving the moisture" — the Bhava Sutta image).
- **Episodes / persons referred to:** the Bodhisatta's learning under **Āḷāra Kālāma** and **Uddaka Rāmaputta** the formless attainments — *ākāsānañcāyatana, viññāṇañcāyatana, ākiñcaññāyatana, nevasaññānāsaññāyatana* — then leaving, since suppressing perception into an *asaññā*/non-perception plane is not cessation but the annihilationist (*uccheda*) extreme.
- Terms used: *diṭṭhi* (view) and *diṭṭha-suta-muta-viññāta*, *maññanā* (conceiving), *viññāṇa-bīja* / *cetanā* / *kamma* / *taṇhā*, *aññamañña-paccaya* (mutual conditionality), *sakkāya-diṭṭhi* (self-view), *anatta*, *saṅkhata* / *asaṅkhata*, *nibbāna* / *parinibbāna*, *sopādisesa* (with residue — the living arahant), *uccheda* / *sassata* (annihilationism / eternalism), *pariharaṇa* (functioning/using). No sutta should be inferred beyond those named.
- **Notes on the source:** a two-speaker session (teacher addresses the questioner as "malli"); **Part 2** of "Seeing the view *as* 'view'." The transcript **duplicates a block** (lines ~127–140 repeat ~101–113). Uses the loom-shuttle, thrown-stone, and dream-double images.

## Main dhamma points
- A two-speaker "good-friend" dialogue on how the arahant still functions but accumulates no kamma, how breath and knowing mutually confirm each other to sustain "I," how the formless attainments fall to the annihilationist extreme, and how the dream-double reveals that "I" is born through seeing and is not-self.
- **The arahant: view seen as view, functioning without accumulating.** In the arahant the "I"-feeling is utterly gone — zero — yet the body, taken up in a past life through clinging to the seen-heard-sensed, still continues; right view does not make the body vanish but purifies the seeing. As a thrown stone travels by its momentum until spent, this body, arisen as the fruit (*vipāka*) of past kamma-consciousness, runs on until that momentum is exhausted, then departs. The arahant eats, drinks, walks, bathes exactly as a worldling, but without the view "a person is doing this"; the worldling functions from "I am doing it — I heard, I ate, it was felt to me," whereas in wisdom it is mere functioning (*pariharaṇa*), with neither "I did" nor "I did not do" formed — so no kamma accumulates, as in the loom the upper thread runs but no lower stitch is struck.
- **Consciousness the seed; the mutual-conditioning knot sustains "I."** *Kammaṁ khettaṁ, viññāṇaṁ bījaṁ, taṇhā sineho*: the consciousness-seed sprung from the view, watered by craving, and it is by volition (*cetanā*) that kamma is fashioned — "I" met, and a seen thing met, and from that the deed. But craving is not a separate extra: when a feeling arises, not seeing it as a view born of mere attention, one takes "a feeling occurs *to someone*," "I arise," "it is felt to *me*," and, holding the view "there is such a thing as feeling," a felt-thing is fabricated for it — that is where perception knots. Seeking "what is felt," "breath" is found; and knotting "breath" is itself what helps volition become kamma. This is *aññamañña-paccaya*, mutual conditioning: "I know I exist because it is felt," and "the feeling is confirmed because breath is really there" — the two proving each other, and by that mutual confirmation it is "I" that is strengthened, bound to saṁsāra by craving and ignorance; not "I stand and tighten them," but by the knot "I" am sustained.
- **This persistence is itself non-persistence.** That very persistence is what "I" is — yet the persistence is itself a non-persistence, a view, the knot struck in not-knowing; seen with wisdom, looking for persistence one finds non-persistence, for "I" never persisted at all, the imagined persisting "I" being an *unpersisting* one. More precisely: "persistence" was made of the two, breath and nose, and that very concept is the sole witness to "I exist," so "I persisted" is only the knot. When self-view is purified — the knot seen as a knot fallen by view, the mutual knotting of knowing and breath seen through — there is no question left of "did I exist or not," that talk being negated, not even needing to be uttered; for to say "I do not exist, I never persisted" is only to take up again the distortion-built "I" and give it an interpretation, a further ignorance-ridden consciousness born to understand the first.
- **The wrong concept merely corrected; "I" dissolves, which is nibbāna.** Nothing is done here: the wrong view "there is feeling" is simply set right — seen that this feeling is only a conceiving, mere attention, not a thing — and, seen without before-and-after, the view "this feeling is not a thing" comes, whereupon "there is one who feels" melts away then and there. That melting is nibbāna: not going to a new place, not a person ceasing, but a distorted personhood, sustained by a wrong view, dissolving — a distortion, a conceiving, a mere notion, an "I" born within the very distortion; when the distortion turns to true-nature, there is no talk of whether things or the self existed or ceased.
- **The formless attainments fall to the annihilationist extreme.** This is why the Bodhisatta left Āḷāra Kālāma and Uddaka Rāmaputta: their highest was *nevasaññānāsaññāyatana*, reached by suppressing perception, taking "when no perception comes, that is cessation" — but that is only the *asaññā* plane, a falling to the annihilationist (*uccheda*), no-existence extreme, from a lack of comprehension of the four (seen-heard-sensed-cognized). The Kalaha-vivāda lines show the arahant's perception instead: *not* perceiving as we do, *not* distorting perception (as when, to escape "chair," one re-names it "four great elements," "pure octads," "electrons and protons, quanta" — another substitute perception, *visaññā*), *not* perception-less (not a dementia-like state where nothing is recognized), and *not* with perception annihilated — neither existent nor non-existent perception.
- **The view is the person; the dream-double shows "I" born through seeing, and not-self.** The view is not something a person sees — the view *is* the person, so the abandoning of the view is the abandoning of the person, of "me"; the "I am"-feeling is the view, and its ceasing is "my" ceasing, accomplished through right view, since the misapprehension in the four is the sole cause the self persists. The dream makes it vivid: the sleeping "me" vanishes and a running, frightened "me" arises in the dream through the very seeing — two "me"s cannot exist at once, and the sleeper is not running, so the dream-"I" is born through seeing, and one cannot control the dream-dog, controlling only through the seeing, never the seeing itself — the mark of not-self, for in an unpersisting, selfless nature no controller can stand. Seeing thus how mighty and dangerous a mere view is — men holding grudges, killing, waging war over it — one sees that if the world could see this, no such warring world would ever be built.

## Summary

This second part of "Seeing the view as view" begins from the arahant, in whom the "I"-feeling is utterly gone yet the body still functions: right view does not make the body vanish but purifies the seeing, and as a thrown stone travels by its momentum until spent, this body, the fruit of past kamma-consciousness, runs on until exhausted, then departs. The arahant eats, walks and bathes exactly as a worldling, but without "a person is doing this" — the worldling acting from "I heard, I ate, it was felt to me," while in wisdom it is mere functioning, with neither "I did" nor "I did not" formed, so no kamma accumulates, as the loom's upper thread runs without a lower stitch. For *kammaṁ khettaṁ, viññāṇaṁ bījaṁ, taṇhā sineho* — the consciousness-seed sprung from the view, watered by craving — and craving is no separate extra: when a feeling arises, not seen as a view born of attention, one takes "it is felt to *me*," fabricates a felt-thing, finds "breath," and knots it, this knotting itself making volition into kamma. This is mutual conditioning: "I exist because it is felt" and "the feeling is real because breath is there," each proving the other, and by that mutual confirmation "I" is strengthened and bound to saṁsāra — not by a standing "I" tightening them, but by the knot sustaining "I."

That very persistence, the teacher shows, is itself a non-persistence, a view, the knot struck in not-knowing; "persistence" was made of breath and nose, the sole witness to "I exist," so "I persisted" is only the knot, and when self-view is purified the very question "did I exist or not" is negated — to say "I never persisted" being only to re-take the distortion-built "I." Nothing is done: the wrong view "there is feeling" is simply corrected, seen to be a mere conceiving, and "there is one who feels" melts away — that melting being nibbāna, not a going or a person ceasing but a distorted personhood dissolving. This is why the Bodhisatta left Āḷāra Kālāma and Uddaka Rāmaputta, whose highest, *nevasaññānāsaññāyatana*, only suppressed perception and fell to the annihilationist extreme; the Kalaha-vivāda lines show the arahant's perception instead — not perceiving as we do, not distorting it (as re-naming a chair "four elements" or "electrons"), not perception-less like dementia, not perception-annihilated. Finally, the view *is* the person, so its abandoning is "my" ceasing, the misapprehension in the four being the sole cause the self persists; and the dream shows it — the sleeper vanishes and a frightened runner arises through the very seeing, two "me"s impossible at once, the dream-"I" born through seeing and unable to control the dream-dog, controlling only through the seeing and never the seeing itself, the mark of not-self — so that, seeing how mighty and dangerous a mere view is, over which men kill and wage war, one sees that if the world could see this, no such warring world would ever arise.
