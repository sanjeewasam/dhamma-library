## Sutta referred to
- Not named by a single discourse-title.
- **The three influxes (āsava)** — named and analysed: *kāmāsava*, *bhavāsava*, *avijjāsava* (sensual, existence, and ignorance influxes).
- Directly quoted / referred to, unattributed: *jāti, jarā, maraṇa, soka, parideva, dukkha, domanassa* (the mass of suffering); *udaya-vaya* / *udaya-vaya-ñāṇa* (rise-and-fall knowledge); *paṭicca-samuppāda* (dependent origination) — "the arising cannot be stopped [while ignorance stands]"; the lotus "in the mud yet unstained."
- Terms used: *āsava* (influx), *diṭṭha-suta-muta-viññāta* (seen-heard-sensed-cognized) and *maññanā* (conceiving), *manasikāra* (attention), *phassa* / *sparśa* (contact), *kāma-taṇhā* / *bhava-taṇhā* (sensual / existence craving), *avijjā* / *moha* (ignorance / delusion), *samudaya* / *nirodha* (arising / cessation), *anutpāda* (non-arising), *arahant*. No sutta should be inferred beyond those named.

## Main dhamma points
- A group "good-friend" dialogue continuing the theme, showing how the present "knowing" fabricates past and future and breeds the influxes, how the three influxes interlink under ignorance, and how the arahant still "associates with" arising while forming no influx — the lotus in the mud.
- **The present knowing fabricates past and future, and breeds the influxes.** Not knowing the knowing is un-knowable lends value to "thinking," and where thinking is valued a "thinker" is built. Because a knowing is met *now*, one can later think "a while ago, when I thought of the breath, breath was felt" — the very fabrication of this present moment being what leaves something to recall in the future, and equally what constructs the "past." An *āsava* is created just here, a mould (*pōra*) prepared for the mind to think again: "I saw such a thing before, I heard such a thing." So past and future are both built from being caught in the middle, and that middle — the "cannot-know" nature, contact (*phassa*) itself — arises only from not-knowing; if the un-buildable-ness of the middle is directly realized, neither middle stands nor either side is built.
- **The middle can't be seen from the two sides; the maker is root delusion.** One cannot see this from the two extremes — past dead, future unborn, and a present between — for looking that way the middle simply disappears when the two are removed; the right way is to see how it arises by condition, whereupon both sides fall away. The middle, seen in the middle, does not really occur, being the not-knowing itself; ignorance is the condition by which both sides were constructed, so the "maker" is root delusion (*mūla-moha*), and seeing root delusion, the two fall away and even the middle finds no footing — "not standing, and not smeared," no middle being met to smear, directly realized by wisdom. Otherwise, not seeing how the two are built by not-knowing, one only tries to strip away the two sides — like locking the stable after the horse has bolted, futilely "breaking" each arising form or sound.
- **The three influxes interlink under ignorance.** Thinking "there is an eye," "there is a chair," is the *kāmāsava*; taking it as really existing is the *bhavāsava*; and taking it all in not-knowing, without knowing the un-knowable nature, is the *avijjāsava* — itself an influx, the root common to all, on which the ladder to the sensual influx is built and from which the existence influx follows, so that if one arises all three arise, the other two like a bonus. Hence, without removing the ignorance-influx, the sensual and existence influxes can never be broken: merely deeming "the chair false" is a futile exercise, for the dependent-origination fabricating cannot be stopped, and however often one re-concludes "it is false," the ignorance-influx is never removed from the mind — attacking the arisen sensual-influx is useless. Only the extraordinary wisdom that sees ignorance *as* ignorance can stop the making; this "cannot" is itself the knowledge, a place consciousness cannot reach or think its way into.
- **Rise-and-fall seen only by one who has seen cessation.** Thinking is what we call rise-and-fall (*udaya-vaya*), and there the talk of "what arises" and "what ceases" both end; rise-and-fall is given us to watch, but the knowledge that comes with it is that this arising-and-ceasing is birth-and-death — and only one who has seen cessation sees rise-and-fall rightly, for "seeing rise-and-fall" is not really seeing a thing arise and cease but seeing the *delusion* of birth-and-death, that nothing actually arises or ceases. Not knowing this, one watches "why the arisen thing ceases just there"; but in truth, on an un-arisen nature, a meeting of an arising-and-ceasing is precisely what saṁsāra is, with its birth, ageing, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain and grief. Seeing that the whole mass is built on a non-arising, one has nothing to do about the "arisen," for the fabricated itself is realized as a non-fabrication, the very forming seen as a non-forming.
- **The arahant associates with arising yet forms no influx — the lotus in the mud.** As a man who employed "Babu" as a servant, later told he is an assassin, must still work alongside him but now inwardly *knows* him for an assassin and so is not caught, the arahant, having seen cessation, must still "associate with" arising — using the four seen-heard-sensed-cognized as they wear away, functioning in the world — yet forms no influx, because the ignorance-influx is gone; having seen the root, seen reality, seen the "cannot" as "cannot," no sensual- or existence-influx arises, and he is in the mud yet unstained, the lotus in the mud. This is why his saṁsāra is shortened; and a mind that has seen ignorance *as* ignorance, established in a cessation-nature, we cannot even imagine or ask how it behaves.

## Summary

This second part continues the group dialogue by showing how the present "knowing" fabricates time and breeds the influxes. Not knowing the knowing is un-knowable lends value to thinking, and where thinking is valued a "thinker" is built; because a knowing is met now, one can later think "a while ago, when I thought of the breath, it was felt," this present fabrication being what leaves something to recall in the future and equally what constructs the past — an influx created just here, a mould for the mind to think again, "I saw such a thing before." So both past and future are built from being caught in the middle, and that middle — the un-knowable nature, contact itself — arises only from not-knowing; if its un-buildable-ness is realized, neither middle stands nor either side is built. One cannot see this from the two extremes, for looking that way the middle merely vanishes when the two are removed; the right way is to see how it arises by condition, whereupon both sides fall, the middle being the not-knowing itself, its maker root delusion — seeing which, the two fall and even the middle finds no footing, "not standing and not smeared." Otherwise one only tries to strip away the two sides, like locking the stable after the horse has bolted.

The three influxes interlink under ignorance: thinking "there is an eye" is the sensual influx, taking it as existing the existence influx, and taking it all in not-knowing the ignorance influx — the root common to all, so that if one arises all three do. Hence, without removing the ignorance-influx the other two can never be broken: deeming "the chair false" is futile, since the dependent-origination fabricating cannot be stopped and the ignorance-influx is never thereby removed; only the extraordinary wisdom that sees ignorance as ignorance can stop the making, this "cannot" being itself the knowledge, unreachable by consciousness. Rise-and-fall, moreover, is seen rightly only by one who has seen cessation, for "seeing rise-and-fall" is not seeing a thing arise and cease but seeing the delusion of birth-and-death — that on an un-arisen nature a meeting of arising-and-ceasing is precisely saṁsāra, with its birth, ageing, death and grief — so that, seeing the whole mass built on a non-arising, one has nothing to do about the "arisen," the fabricated realized as a non-fabrication. Finally, as a man who keeps employing a servant he now knows to be an assassin is not caught by him, the arahant, having seen cessation, still "associates with" arising, using the four as they wear away, yet forms no influx because the ignorance-influx is gone — in the mud yet unstained, the lotus in the mud — which shortens his saṁsāra, and a mind that has seen ignorance as ignorance we cannot even imagine.
