## Sutta referred to
- Directly quoted / referred to, unattributed by title: the twelve links of dependent origination — *avijjā-paccayā saṅkhārā, saṅkhāra-paccayā viññāṇaṁ, viññāṇa-paccayā nāma-rūpaṁ, … saḷāyatana, phassa, vedanā, taṇhā, upādāna, bhava, jāti*; the *sabbe dhammā* formulas — *phassa-samudayā sabbe dhammā* / *phassa-mūlakā sabbe dhammā* / *chanda-mūlakā sabbe dhammā* / *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā* ("all things originate from contact / are rooted in contact / rooted in desire / arise from attention," the **Mūlaka / Kiṁmūlaka Sutta**, AN 8.83 / 10.58); **the Majjhima Sutta** — "contact one end, the arising of contact one end, the cessation of contact the middle."
- Referred to, unattributed: the twelve links likened to fruits hanging from a single mango-stalk (all hanging from *avijjā*); a parable of a man **searching under a lamp** for what he lost in the dark; *saññā* as a mirage; the chicken-and-egg regress.
- Terms used: *phassa* / *sparśa* (contact), *avijjā* (ignorance), *saṅkhāra*, *viññāṇa*, *nāma-rūpa*, *saḷāyatana* (six bases), *vedanā*, *taṇhā*, *upādāna*, *bhava*, *jāti*; *udaya-vaya* (rise-and-fall), *saññā* (perception), *ñāṇa* (knowledge), *viññāṇa-agocara* (not within consciousness's range), *chanda* (desire), *manasikāra* (attention). No sutta should be inferred beyond those named.

## Main dhamma points
- The closing segment of the second Kandy discussion, showing that we search for the cause in the wrong place, that all twelve links hang from the un-cognizable "burning" that is contact, and that when wisdom rightly settles at contact no cause arises and the whole cause-and-fruit chain collapses.
- **We search for the cause in the lit place, but it lies in the dark.** Examining the cause-and-fruit of the built story is fruitless — hen and egg endlessly — for "burning" itself is the egg, the incident; unable to grasp "burning," we look for a cause, "oil," and asking "how does it burn?" we answer "oil burns," from which a fruit, the flame, arises, and then we watch the two like hen and egg. Both "it began" and "the flame ended" are perceptions — arising a perception, ceasing a perception — and not knowing perception is a mirage, we keep watching "arose / ceased," the rise-and-fall game that never ends. It is like a man who drops something in the dark and searches under a lamp because there is light there: our problem is in the dark, the cause in the unseen, and we search in the lit, cognizable place — which is why it can never be grasped in life.
- **All twelve links hang from the un-cognizable "burning" that is contact.** We take the fabricated cause and fruit and try to watch how dependent origination "arises," but the arising is not within consciousness's range and is never understood by the mind — and it is precisely *because* it is not understood that *avijjā-paccayā saṅkhārā, saṅkhāra-paccayā viññāṇaṁ, viññāṇa-paccayā nāma-rūpaṁ*, and so on through the six bases, contact, feeling, craving, clinging, existence, birth. The whole twelvefold process arises upon this not-knowing at the root — like fruits hanging from a single mango-stalk, all twelve hang from the one stalk of *avijjā*, the not-knowing that is exactly the un-cognizable "burning," contact; and because the cause can never be understood, one can never cross *saṁsāra* by consciousness.
- **Contact is not a thing; it is built from not-knowing and runs through the whole chain.** One must not now take "there is a contact, and from it this happens" — contact is not a thing that exists, and *phassa* itself is fashioned from not-knowing. This is why *phassa-samudayā sabbe dhammā*, *phassa-mūlakā sabbe dhammā*, *chanda-mūlakā sabbe dhammā*, *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā* — all things originate from contact, are rooted in contact, rooted in desire, and arise from attention; it is by attention that all dhammas are fashioned, with desire as their root, today's very liking the root from which all dhammas now arise. So one must not seize contact as a fact from which things proceed — that too is a knot; untying everything, at the last one is caught by contact, which is why the Majjhima Sutta says "contact one end, the arising of contact one end, the cessation of contact the middle." Contact runs through the whole of dependent origination like its cancer.
- **Where wisdom rightly settles at contact, no cause arises and the chain collapses.** We knot on the cause "oil" because "burning" alone cannot stand without a cause, so we attach one; and attaching a cause, a fruit inevitably arises — it cannot be stopped. But "oil" does not belong to "burning"; so at the place where "oil" does not apply — the burning, an act un-cognizable to the mind — if wisdom settles *there*, no cause ever arises, and this is what knowledge is: coming to the state where causes do not arise. Causes not arising means the "a fruit arises" talk is over, and the whole cause-and-fruit process breaks apart — if wisdom rightly settles at contact. How that wisdom is to be established is what we now take up as a "method"; up to this point it is clear. (The segment ends here, on "[TO BE CONTINUED].")

## Summary

This closing segment shows that we search for the cause in the wrong place. Examining the cause-and-fruit of the built story is fruitless — hen and egg endlessly — for "burning" is the egg, the incident; unable to grasp it, we look for a cause, "oil," and asking "how does it burn?" answer "oil burns," a flame arising as fruit, then watch the two like hen and egg. Both "it began" and "the flame ended" are perceptions, and not knowing perception is a mirage we keep watching "arose / ceased," the rise-and-fall game without end — like a man who drops something in the dark and searches under a lamp because there is light there, while our problem is in the dark, the cause unseen, and we search in the lit place, which is why it can never be grasped. We take the fabricated cause and fruit and try to watch dependent origination arise, but the arising is not within consciousness's range and is never understood by the mind — and it is because it is not understood that *avijjā-paccayā saṅkhārā*, and so on through the six bases, contact, feeling, craving, clinging, existence, birth; the whole twelvefold hangs, like fruits from one mango-stalk, on the single stalk of *avijjā*, the not-knowing that is the un-cognizable "burning," contact, so that one can never cross *saṁsāra* by consciousness.

Contact is not a thing that exists; *phassa* itself is fashioned from not-knowing, which is why *phassa-samudayā sabbe dhammā*, *phassa-mūlakā sabbe dhammā*, *chanda-mūlakā sabbe dhammā*, *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā* — all things originate from contact, are rooted in contact and in desire, and arise from attention, today's very liking their root. So one must not seize contact as a fact from which things proceed — that too is a knot, and untying everything one is caught at the last by contact, which is why the Majjhima Sutta says "contact one end, its arising one end, its cessation the middle"; contact runs through the whole chain like its cancer. We knot on "oil" because "burning" cannot stand without a cause, and attaching a cause a fruit inevitably arises; but oil does not belong to burning, so at the place where oil does not apply — the burning, un-cognizable to the mind — if wisdom settles there, no cause arises, which is what knowledge is: the state where causes do not arise, the "a fruit arises" talk over, the whole cause-and-fruit process broken apart. How that wisdom is established is what is taken up next as a "method." (This is Part 4, the close of "Nuwara [Kandy] Dhamma Discussion 2"; it ends on "[TO BE CONTINUED].")
