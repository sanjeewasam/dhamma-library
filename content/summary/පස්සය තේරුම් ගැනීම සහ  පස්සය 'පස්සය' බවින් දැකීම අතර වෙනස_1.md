## Sutta referred to
- Named / clearly alluded to: the **Aggi-Vacchagotta Sutta (MN 72)** — fire arising "because of firewood," the four questions to the wanderer Vacchagotta, and the gone-out fire asked "which direction did it go — north, east, west, south?"; *sotañ ca paṭicca sadde ca uppajjati sota-viññāṇaṁ, tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso* ("dependent on the ear and sounds, ear-consciousness arises; the meeting of the three is contact" — the **Madhupiṇḍika / Saḷāyatana** formula); *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā* ("all things arise from attention" — the **Mūlaka / Kiṁmūlaka Sutta**, AN 8.83 / 10.58).
- Referred to, unattributed: the **dress-and-stitching** image and the **mind-and-mental-object** (*mano* / *dhamma*) contact from a written letter being read aloud; the "**magician at the four-way crossroads**"; a **reflection** (*pratibimba*) in water.
- Terms used: *phassa* (contact), *paccaya* / *pratyaya-vīma* (condition / being-conditioned), *ārammaṇa* (object), *manasikāra* (attention), *maññanā* (conceiving), *viññāṇa* / *sota-viññāṇa* (consciousness / ear-consciousness), *vidyamāna* (manifest), *pera pasu novī* (not before, not after), *dhammatā* (natural law). No sutta should be inferred beyond those named.
- **Notes on the source:** the **first segment (Part 1)** of a **three-part discussion, "The Difference Between Understanding Contact and Seeing Contact as 'Contact'"** (*Passaya tērum-gänīma saha passaya 'passaya' bavin däkīma atara venasa*) — a two-speaker dialogue (teacher and a student reading from a letter). It works the fire-and-firewood simile, the ear-and-sound formula, and a reflection in water. The segment ends on "[TO BE CONTINUED]."

## Main dhamma points
- The first segment, showing that a conditioned thing has no findable root, that "because-of" is applied only where it cannot be applied, that consciousness is what knots the two into a "contact," and that the conditioned "manifests" like a reflection that is in neither the tree nor the water.
- **A conditioned thing has no findable root, so birth and death are transcended.** As the Buddha showed the wanderer Vacchagotta, fire arises "because of firewood," yet fire has no separate existence and is not *in* the wood; the two knotting yields a "persistence" called fire, but no source can be pointed to, and asked which way the gone-out fire went — north, east, west, south — there is no arriving-place and no going-place, hence no root, no birth, and so no death: birth and death are both transcended. Fire's persistence comes only from the two mutually grasping — firewood grasping fire, fire grasping firewood — which is an impossible thing that only seems to happen, so one can never say "it came from this," and the "because-of" is applied exactly where it cannot be applied. This is un-cognizable to consciousness, a place where consciousness only *seems* to settle though it cannot; we apply "because-of" only because we do not know it cannot be applied — being-conditioned happens only through not knowing it — and were it known, no knot could form.
- **An object arising is what makes "a mind exists" register; neither has a root.** An object's arising (*ārammaṇa*) is what makes "a mind exists" register, and before an object arises one can never register a mind, nor without a mind register an object — "not before, not after" (the *dhammatā*), for the object's arising *is* the mind's arising, *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā*. One cannot say mind-because-of-mind produced the object, nor object-because-of produced the mind, nor separate them; and since it could not be said before, it cannot be said now, so where no source is ever found there is truly no root — the subtle point — neither mind nor object having a root. How then is a rootless thing "there" now? It merely *manifests* (*vidyamāna*), appearing to exist though it cannot, *after* "arising."
- **Consciousness is what knots the two into a contact; hence no fault is findable.** Once object and mind arise, the object can no more be faulted than fire from wood: the mind is witness to the object, the object makes the mind real, and because a mind registers, the object cannot be called false — it came to a mind — so from no side can it be falsified once the knot has fallen, which is the magician at the four-way crossroads, the magic itself "real," every angle confirming "it is." So too with sound and ear: "a sound" registers only if a hearing arose, there is no reason to think an ear pre-exists while no sound arises, and one can say neither "the ear exists" nor "the sound exists," each making the other register, mutually conditioned — *sotañ ca paṭicca sadde ca uppajjati sota-viññāṇaṁ*, the meeting (*saṅgati*) veiling the not-knowing. Consciousness is precisely what knots the two by "because-of" — connecting *is* knotting — and the knotting is what draws consciousness into the story, consciousness not knowing it is a place where no knot can be tied.
- **"Attention" has no connection to ear or sound; knowing this, the whole fabrication would never form.** When conceiving (*maññanā*) arises, the "ear and sound" talk arises; a hearing arising *is* "ear and sound" registering, and "ear and sound" registering *is* "a mind exists" registering — both sides through the thinking, *manasikāra-sambhavo*. Yet "attention" has no connection to ear or sound at all; if a mind cognized attention *as* attention, this "ear-and-sound-so-heard" fabrication would never form — one would never think "an ear exists, the birds' sounds outside come and strike my ear," that talk simply would not arise. This is why being-conditioned is never understood: understanding itself *is* consciousness, whose meaning is knowing, so the two knotted by "because-of" make the mind real and the ear-consciousness real, which then makes "ear and sound" real again, and no fault is ever findable — understanding produces the "understander," recognizing arises the "recognizer."
- **The conditioned "manifests" like a reflection in neither the tree nor the water.** An object has a reflection only with the object, yet the reflection is not *in* the object and has no place: a tree by a river casts a reflection that seems to persist in the water, but scoop the water into a vessel and the reflection does not come, so one cannot say for sure it is in the water — it can be given no location — nor is it in the tree, for examine the tree and no reflection is found, examine the water and none is found. That is what "manifesting" means: a persistence appears where nothing can persist, seeming to exist. Likewise the "seer" arising through "seeing," or the "feeler" through "feeling" — if from morning one was not feeling the breath or the ground, one can go to neither "I felt" nor "I did not feel," and cannot say through feeling whether a feeling-"me" existed; we say "it was not arisen" only because it is needed here, meaning the feeling "was not manifest," and precisely because we abide *within* feeling it does not show. So when feeling suddenly begins to register we say "I did not feel it from morning," yet "from morning" cannot be connected with "feeling," for from morning one abode in not-knowing — and thus we fabricate a past that feeling cannot connect with, taking a never-met feeling in the mode of absence. (The segment breaks off here, on "[TO BE CONTINUED].")

## Summary

This first segment shows that a conditioned thing has no findable root. As the Buddha showed Vacchagotta, fire arises "because of firewood," yet fire is not in the wood, and the two knotting yields a "persistence" with no source to point to; asked which way the gone-out fire went, there is no arriving- or going-place, hence no root, no birth, no death — both transcended. Fire's persistence comes only from the two mutually grasping, an impossible thing that only seems to happen, so "it came from this" can never be said, and "because-of" is applied exactly where it cannot be, un-cognizable to consciousness, which only seems to settle there; we apply it only because we do not know it cannot be applied, and were it known, no knot would form. An object's arising is what makes "a mind exists" register, and neither can be registered before the other — "not before, not after," the object's arising being the mind's arising, *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā* — so neither has a root, and a rootless thing merely *manifests*, appearing to exist though it cannot.

Once object and mind arise, the object can no more be faulted than fire from wood: the mind witnesses the object, the object makes the mind real, and because a mind registers the object cannot be called false, so from no side is it falsifiable once the knot falls — the magician at the crossroads, the magic "real." So with sound and ear: "a sound" registers only if a hearing arose, and one can say neither "the ear exists" nor "the sound exists," each making the other register — *sotañ ca paṭicca sadde ca uppajjati sota-viññāṇaṁ*, the meeting veiling the not-knowing — consciousness being what knots the two by "because-of," the knotting drawing it into the story. When conceiving arises the "ear and sound" talk arises, and a hearing arising *is* "ear and sound" registering, which *is* "a mind exists" registering, all through the thinking; yet "attention" has no connection to ear or sound, and if a mind cognized attention as attention this whole fabrication would never form. This is why being-conditioned is never understood: understanding itself *is* consciousness, whose meaning is knowing, so the knot makes the mind and ear-consciousness real, which makes "ear and sound" real again, and no fault is findable — understanding produces the understander. And the conditioned manifests like a reflection in neither the tree nor the water: a tree's reflection seems to persist in the river, but scoop the water and it does not come, nor is it in the tree — a persistence appearing where nothing can persist. Likewise the feeler through feeling: abiding *within* feeling it does not show, so we fabricate "I did not feel it from morning," connecting "from morning" with a feeling it can never connect with, taking a never-met feeling in the mode of absence. (This is Part 1 of "The Difference Between Understanding Contact and Seeing Contact as 'Contact'"; it ends on "[TO BE CONTINUED].")
