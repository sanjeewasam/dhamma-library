## Sutta referred to
- Named / clearly quoted: **the Majjhima Sutta** — "contact (*phassa*) is one end, the arising of contact (*phassa-samudaya*) is one end, the cessation of contact (*phassa-nirodha*) is the middle" (with "not smeared with the middle"); *cakkhuñ ca paṭicca rūpe ca uppajjati cakkhu-viññāṇaṁ, tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso* ("dependent on the eye and forms, eye-consciousness arises; the meeting of the three is contact" — the **Madhupiṇḍika / Saḷāyatana** formula); *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā* ("all things arise from attention," the **Mūlaka / Kiṁmūlaka Sutta**); *sabbe saṅkhārā aniccā'ti, yadā paññāya passati* ("all formations are impermanent — when one sees this with wisdom," **Dhammapada 277**); the **Four Noble Truths** (seeing the arising, cessation is realized).
- Referred to, unattributed: the **two reed-bundles** (*naḷakalāpī*, **SN 12.67**); a **matchbox and matchstick** producing fire by "striking"; *phassaṁ phassena passati* (seeing contact by contact).
- Terms used: *phassa* / *phassa-samudaya* / *phassa-nirodha* (contact / its arising / its cessation), *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati* (meeting of three), *anuppāda* (non-arising), *bhava* (existence), *viññāṇa* / *paññā* (consciousness / wisdom), *viññāṇa-agocara* (not within consciousness's range), *manasikāra* (attention), *saṅkhāra-dukkha* (suffering of formations), *pañca-upādāna-skandha* (five clinging-aggregates). ## Main dhamma points
- The first segment, using the Majjhima Sutta's "contact one end, its arising one end, its cessation the middle" to show that taking contact as a separate thing and taking it as the three are both extremes, that contact is the un-cognizable "striking," and that understanding it only builds a fresh knot rather than seeing it through.
- **Both "contact is separate" and "contact is the three" are extremes.** Taking "there is a contact, and everything happens from it" makes contact an end and gives it an existence (*bhava*), yet no contact can be shown to exist separately, just as the two reed-bundles show that no "cause" stands apart. And taking the three — feeling, nose, breath — as "contact," or contact as merely "produced by the three being present," is the *phassa-samudaya* end: for the three are not contact (one cannot call feeling or nose or breath "contact"), and if their mere presence produced contact it would be happening constantly and we would feel constantly, which we do not. So contact is not from the things.
- **Contact is the un-cognizable "striking"; consciousness itself arises from it.** As a matchbox and matchstick merely present are not the "cause" — we name them cause only because fire arises — so fire arises not from the two being present but from the *act of striking*, which is contact; and "striking" is in neither the box, the stick, nor the fire, yet the cause (box-and-stick) and the fruit (fire) are set up by it. So contact is the striking, the *thinking*, that divides nose-and-breath from feeling into "the three," and speaking of *samudaya* at all is only "because of contact," which builds the two sides while itself manifesting only through them — the real magic. Examining "contact is not the three" one slides to "contact has no connection to them, so it is a separate thing" — the other end — and consciousness can never cognize contact, precisely because consciousness itself arises from this very thinking: *cakkhuñ ca paṭicca rūpe ca uppajjati cakkhu-viññāṇaṁ*, consciousness arising by grasping two conditions, so it can never grasp that "it was by thinking that both I and the eye-and-form became real," *manasikāra-sambhavā sabbe dhammā*.
- **The knot cannot be falsified, because the falsifier is born of it.** Seeing arises, real and undeniable; to justify it, eye and form are real and cannot be denied since it is seen; one cannot remove "seeing" while "eye and form exist" registers, nor call eye-and-form false while the "seeing" view stands — so consciousness never grasps that seeing arose from a not-knowing, from the cessation-nature things eye-and-form. Understanding *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso* only builds another *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati*: analyzing "the eye is such, form is such" makes the five clinging-aggregates real, and then declaring "it is false, a mirage" makes "false" itself real — as re-hearing a false report only re-fixes it differently — so understanding "eye and form are un-arisen, seeing is un-arisen" is caught in contact again, a fresh meeting-of-three forming from fresh mind-objects, described with fresh perceptions under the name "Dhamma."
- **Understanding is not seeing-through; it builds the understander, so *saṅkhāra-dukkha* is un-cognizable.** The Buddha gave *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso* to be *seen through* — its non-arising realized — not merely understood; checked against the Four Noble Truths, seeing the *samudaya* the *nirodha* should be realized, yet going to see the *samudaya*, from the not-understanding a fresh *samudaya* is built (a fresh "this is wrong, a mirage"), so cessation is not realized. Understanding produces the "understander," and understanding itself is built from contact, which is why *saṅkhāra-dukkha* can never be understood: the constructing, rooted in contact, is un-cognizable to consciousness, not within its range — which is exactly why the Buddha said *sabbe saṅkhārā aniccā'ti, yadā paññāya passati*, to be seen with *wisdom*, not consciousness; and at least this much must be grasped as a point, to know the Dhamma's true nature.
