## Sutta referred to
- Referred to, unattributed: the rebirth answer "**not the one who dies, yet not another**" (neither "the same man reborn" — eternalism — nor "another" — annihilation; the *avyākata* tetralemma, resonant with **SN 44 / the Milindapañha**); *avijjā-paccayā saṅkhārā*, the dependent-origination links *saṅkhāra, viññāṇa, nāma-rūpa*; the Majjhima Sutta's "contact one end, its arising one end, its cessation the middle"; *pubbe ananussutesu dhammesu* (things not heard before, that no teacher pointed to contact).
- Referred to, unattributed: the **two reed-bundles** (*naḷakalāpī*); a **matchbox and matchstick** producing fire by "striking"; a **pothole** on the road pointed out to avoid; *phassaṁ phassena passati* (seeing contact by contact); *upekkhā*.
- Terms used: *phassa* / *phassa-samudaya* / *phassa-nirodha* (contact / its arising / its cessation), *paccayā* ("because-of," being-conditioned), *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso* (meeting-of-three contact), *sakkāya-diṭṭhi* (self-view), *atthi* / *natthi* / *uccheda* ("is" / "is-not" / annihilation), *adhicca-samuppanna* (uncaused, "spontaneous"), *viññāṇa* / *paññā* (consciousness / wisdom), *viññāṇa-agocara* (not within consciousness's range), *anuppāda* (non-arising). No sutta should be inferred beyond those named.
- **Notes on the source:** the **second segment (Part 2)** of **"Contact and the Arising of Contact — Falling into the Extremes"** — a two-speaker dialogue. A forgetting-and-remembering illustration closes it. **Transcription artifact to flag:** the forgetting/remembering exchange near the end is duplicated verbatim (a looped block).

## Main dhamma points
- The second segment, showing that "because-of" is un-cognizable so we grasp only the two sides, that contact is the un-born author of the whole story from which the self arises, and that "is" and "is-not" arise together — as forgetting and remembering do — so the world's "is-not" is only another "is."
- **Taking contact "in the causes" re-fixes it by ignorance; taking it "apart" makes it uncaused — both extremes.** The cause-and-fruit law is constructed only because contact ("because-of") is not seen; so going back to "study" that law by placing contact *in* the causes only re-binds one to the cause, making it permanent again through ignorance, and no contact can touch a cause when rightly seen. Yet concluding "contact stands apart, free of the causes" makes it uncaused, "spontaneous" (*adhicca-samuppanna*) — the other end — for we can say only "because of the causes" or "not because of the causes," the two limits consciousness can think. This is the Buddha's rebirth answer, "not the one who dies, yet not another": logic must land on "the same man reborn" (eternalism) or "another" (annihilation), but the Buddha transcends both, so birth cannot be spoken — which is *nirodha*.
- **Contact is the un-born author of the story from which the self arises.** Contact has no analysis and no place; it is un-arisen, and from that un-cognizable, un-born place the whole story arises, and with it *sakkāya-diṭṭhi*: dependent on body and tangible, "feeling" arises, and with the feeling the feeler is born, with hearing the hearer — so the person arises at the very *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati*, contact being the author of the whole tale. Because consciousness itself arises from this event, contact can never be cognized by consciousness, one can never grasp its own root, never see the fault of *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati*, and never prove "the world is false" — for the mind is constructed *from tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati*, and whatever the mind is built from registers as real. Told "the Buddha said *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso*," people only rote-learn the escape-instruction, as one told "there is a pothole ahead, watch out" is meant to avoid it, not memorize and fall in; the Buddha pointed out the very trap we are caught in so as to escape it, and since no teacher before him ever pointed to contact (*pubbe ananussuta*), their highest goal is only a heaven — a construction — unable to show escape from constructing itself.
- **We grasp only the two sides, never the "because-of"; hence the middle is never thought.** The whole of dependent origination hangs on "because-of," which links it all; but since "because-of" is un-cognizable to consciousness, we grasp only the two sides — *saṅkhāra*, *viññāṇa*, *nāma-rūpa*, the consciousness-cognizable *dhammas*, words to which we can give meaning — and never the connecting middle, which can be given no meaning (matchbox-and-stick "because-of" fire has no meaning of its own). So consciousness snaps up "the two knotted things and the fire," never the knotting; the two ends, contact and its arising, are all it can reach, and only seeing neither — where neither "because of this" nor "not because of this" applies to that nature, when ignorance is rightly seen — is the middle, *nirodha*, the seeing of equanimity.
- **"Is" and "is-not" arise together, as forgetting and remembering do.** No idea "feeling because of nose-and-breath" arises on its own, nor does "there is no breath" register on its own; both need the knot, the two striking together, and we can conceive the "is-not" extreme only by taking up the met causes — so the "is-not" does not really exist or get met, and until one sees that annihilation (*uccheda*) is never met, one cannot rightly see "it exists" either, for "there is" gets its very meaning from "the absence is not met." Passing unheeded things on the road, one never registers "a shop I do not see"; "forgotten" is itself another remembering, and forgetting and remembering arise together — "I forgot, now I remember" — yet seeing both arise together now, what was "forgotten"? nothing sayable. So too "is" and "is-not" arise together, and the world's handling of "is-not" is only another "is"; seen thus, "is-not" loses its meaning, and the meaning "there is" carried all this while dissolves with it. (The segment ends on this forgetting/remembering point, which the transcript repeats verbatim.)

## Summary

This second segment shows that "because-of" is un-cognizable, so we grasp only the two sides. The cause-and-fruit law is constructed only because contact is not seen, so placing contact *in* the causes only re-binds one through ignorance, making the cause permanent again, while placing it *apart* makes it uncaused, "spontaneous" — for we can say only "because of the causes" or "not because of them," the two limits consciousness can think. This is the Buddha's "not the one who dies, yet not another": logic must land on eternalism or annihilation, but he transcends both, so birth cannot be spoken, which is *nirodha*. Contact has no analysis and no place, is un-arisen, and from that un-cognizable place the whole story arises with *sakkāya-diṭṭhi* — feeling arising with the feeler, the person born at the very *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati*, contact the author of the tale. Because consciousness itself arises from this, it can never cognize contact, grasp its own root, see the fault of the meeting-of-three, or prove "the world is false," for the mind is built from *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati* and whatever it is built from registers as real. Told "the Buddha said *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso*," people only rote-learn the escape-instruction, as one told of a pothole is meant to avoid it, not fall in; the Buddha pointed out the very trap to escape it, and since no teacher before him pointed to contact, their highest goal is only a construction, unable to show escape from constructing.

The whole of dependent origination hangs on "because-of," but since it is un-cognizable we grasp only the two sides — formations, consciousness, name-and-form, the words we can give meaning — never the connecting middle, which has no meaning of its own (matchbox-and-stick "because-of" fire); so consciousness snaps up the two knotted things and the fire, never the knotting, and only seeing neither — where neither "because of this" nor "not because of this" applies, when ignorance is rightly seen — is the middle, *nirodha*, the seeing of equanimity. And "is" and "is-not" arise together: no "feeling because of nose-and-breath" arises alone, nor does "there is no breath," both needing the knot, so the "is-not" is conceived only by taking up the met causes and never really met — and until one sees that annihilation is never met, one cannot rightly see "it exists," for "there is" gets its meaning from "the absence is not met." Passing unheeded things one never registers "a shop I do not see"; "forgotten" is itself another remembering, and forgetting and remembering arise together — "I forgot, now I remember" — yet seeing both arise together now, what was forgotten is nothing sayable; so too "is" and "is-not" arise together, the world's "is-not" only another "is," and seen thus "is-not" loses its meaning and the meaning "there is" dissolves with it.
