## Sutta referred to
- Directly quoted / referred to, unattributed by title: *sotañ ca paṭicca sadde ca uppajjati sota-viññāṇaṁ, tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso* ("dependent on the ear and sounds, ear-consciousness arises; the meeting of the three is contact" — the **Saḷāyatana / Madhupiṇḍika** formula); form as a mirage/lump-of-foam, feeling as a water-bubble, perception as a mirage (the **Pheṇa-piṇḍūpama, SN 22.95**); the plantain-trunk (*kadalikkhandha*) with no heartwood, as the coreless nature of formations (same sutta); the five clinging-aggregates *rūpa-vedanā-saññā-saṅkhāra-viññāṇa*.
- Named modern teacher: **S. N. Goenka** — credited with the two-strands-twisted-into-a-rope simile (the speaker reworks it).
- Referred to, unattributed: *phassaṁ phassena passati* (seeing contact by contact) and *phassa-nirodha*; *dhammānupassanā*; the "functional minds" (*kriyā-citta*) said to belong to arahants; a **shadow** one cannot escape; *sakkāya-diṭṭhi* (self-view).
- Terms used: *phassa* / *kriyā* (contact / action), *tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso*, *saṁsandana* (comparison), *dvayatā* (duality), *manasikāra* / *maññanā* (attention / conceiving), *viññāṇa* / *sota-viññāṇa* / *mano-viññāṇa*, *saṅkhāra* (formations, *asāra* / coreless), *pañca-upādāna-skandha*, *sakkāya*, *anicca* (impermanence), *māyā* (illusion), *sammā-jāti* (the true manner of birth). No sutta should be inferred beyond those named.
- **Notes on the source:** a **single self-contained talk, "The Cognizing That Arises From Contact — Can It Be Transcended by Understanding?"** — a dialogue between the teacher and an elder laywoman (addressed as *Anti*, "Auntie"). Illustrations: equal-height trees, a matchbox and fire, the plantain-trunk, two strands twisted into a rope, and a shadow.

## Main dhamma points
- A single talk answering its own title — that contact-born cognizing cannot be transcended by understanding — showing that "comparison" is the un-locatable contact, that no word or "mind" is a real thing, and that understanding only builds a fresh consciousness, so that only seeing contact by contact frees.
- **"Comparison" is the un-locatable contact; the action can be called neither a thing nor a mind.** The un-cognizable *action* is contact; not knowing it un-findable, we knot two things (an ear, a sound) to make sense of the "hearing," and with the hearing the hearer arises. If every tree in the world were of equal height, one could say neither "tall" nor "short" — no duality, so no condition for a thought; to think, the mind must seize a second, a comparison, and only when one tree stands unequal does "tall/short" arise. So the two became *two* only from an un-findable "comparison" that is nowhere in the trees — and that comparison *is* contact: it is in neither the equal trees nor the odd one, un-locatable, so seeing it one can say neither "contact exists" nor "contact is a mind," and its very non-establishment is realized, which is seeing contact by contact, the cessation of contact. The matchbox shows the same: fire arises not from box-and-stick being present but from the *striking*, which is in neither box, stick, nor fire — every action is thus nowhere-located, so no consciousness can be loaded onto it (consciousness needs a thing to settle on), and not even the "functional minds" of arahants can truly be spoken of, for the mind itself is the illusion.
- **No word or "mind" is a real thing; formations are coreless.** Our deepest fault is that we take *every word* as a thing: "chair" has no shape or colour in it, a child taught "chair" gets no shape from the word, and to a foreigner it means nothing — "chair" is empty of "chair," and seen *by* chair nothing findable is met, neither met nor not-met. Like the plantain-trunk peeled layer by layer with no heartwood, formations have no core (*asāra*), only "moving this and that" — which is the true meaning of *saṅkhāra*; and "thinking" itself is not a mind, for we suppose we have a mind only *because of* the action of thinking, the mind arising *with* the attention (*manasikāra*) as *sota-viññāṇa*, and with it the hearer arising like the reflection of hearing.
- **The self is the shadow of hearing, un-falsifiable; the whole aggregate-knot becomes "me."** As one cannot escape one's shadow while one stands, so while "hearing" is real "the hearer," its reflection, is there and cannot be falsified — and as long as consciousness arises, "I" arise, again and again. Hearing is not something a "someone" receives; it is only hearing, with no separate receiver, yet we live in "I get a hearing," looking from the wrong side, and because the hearer arises *from* hearing one can never fault hearing (one's very birth is from it) or prove it false, this true manner of birth being un-searchable, findable in no other way. Once "hearing and I" arise, to confirm them a further knot forms — "*my* ear heard the sound" — ear and sound, two perceptions, knotted to make hearing real, neither deniable; then thoughts spin out ("a bird? a vehicle? a three-wheeler? a bus?"), and the whole five clinging-aggregates assemble, all knotted by consciousness and all become "me" — form is me, feeling me, perception me, formations me, consciousness me — the "I"-reflection arising from the very hearing, so that consciousness can never grasp or remove this view, which itself arises from consciousness, as one cannot erase a shadow while standing.
- **Understanding only builds a fresh consciousness; the two strands and the rope.** This is why the cognizing cannot be transcended by understanding: we look at the fault of the *understood* (ear and sound registering), but "the understanding *is* the fault" cannot be understood, for understanding it is another consciousness. Understanding "consciousness is false, form a mirage, feeling a bubble" is itself mind-object and mind-consciousness — the mind made real, the thinker made real, mind-objects made real — and self-identity arises again, like a restless child caught whichever way it turns; so the Dhamma too must be let go, and when contemplation of *dhammas* matures the *dhammas* themselves must be seen through, since taking "these arise from two things, eye and form" is only seizing two more things and holding a thought as existing where nothing persists, from which "mind and thought" and again "I" and the view arise. As Goenka's image has it, two strands twisted the *same* way make no rope, but when a third grips the middle with opposing force a rope forms — and the onlooker who watched from the start sees "there was no rope; the gripping made it," which is one who *sees contact*; a latecomer sees only the finished rope, and unable to reach the middle he never knew of, can only burn it or call it "false, a mirage, a dream, impermanent" — "the world is impermanent" from those who never saw contact — yet the burnt rope remains as a residual formation, and he is not freed, whereas the one who saw the twisting knows how the rope arose, has nothing to destroy and nothing to hold. So "I understood it" is not seeing-through but only one more cognized level, building yet more consciousness — and to see *that*, that "I understood" is only a consciousness-level, is itself enough.

## Summary

This single talk answers its own title: contact-born cognizing cannot be transcended by understanding, only by seeing it as only-consciousness. The un-cognizable action is contact; not knowing it un-findable, we knot an ear and a sound to make sense of "hearing," and with the hearing the hearer arises. Were all trees equal in height, one could say neither "tall" nor "short" — no duality, no condition for thought; to think, the mind seizes a comparison, and only an odd tree yields "tall/short," so the two became two only from an un-findable comparison nowhere in the trees — and that comparison *is* contact, in neither the equal trees nor the odd one, so one can say neither "contact exists" nor "contact is a mind," its non-establishment being the cessation of contact. The matchbox shows the same: fire arises from the striking, in neither box, stick, nor fire, so no consciousness can be loaded onto an action, and even the arahants' "functional minds" cannot truly be spoken of, the mind itself being the illusion. Our deepest fault is taking every word as a thing: "chair" has no shape in it, means nothing to a foreigner, is empty of "chair," and formations are coreless like a plantain-trunk with no heartwood — while "thinking" is not a mind, for we suppose a mind only *because of* the action of thinking, the mind arising with the attention as *sota-viññāṇa*, the hearer arising like hearing's reflection.

As one cannot escape one's shadow while one stands, so while hearing is real the hearer, its reflection, is there and cannot be falsified, and as long as consciousness arises "I" arise; hearing is not something a someone receives but only hearing, yet we live in "I get a hearing," and because the hearer arises *from* hearing one can never fault hearing or prove it false, one's very birth being from it. To confirm "hearing and I," a further knot — "my ear heard the sound" — forms, two perceptions knotted, then thoughts spin out and the whole five clinging-aggregates assemble, all becoming "me," the "I"-reflection arising from the hearing, so consciousness can never remove this view, which itself arises from consciousness. This is why understanding cannot transcend it: "the understanding is the fault" cannot be understood, for understanding it is another consciousness; understanding "consciousness is false, form a mirage, feeling a bubble" is mind-object and mind-consciousness, self-identity arising again, so the Dhamma too must be let go and the *dhammas* seen through, since "these arise from eye and form" seizes two more things. As Goenka's two strands twisted the same way make no rope, but a third gripping the middle with opposing force makes one, the onlooker who watched from the start sees "the gripping made it" — seeing contact — while a latecomer sees only the finished rope and can but burn it or call it "impermanent, a mirage," the burnt rope remaining a formation, unfreed; the one who saw the twisting has nothing to destroy and nothing to hold. So "I understood it" is not seeing-through but one more cognized level building further consciousness — and to see *that* is itself enough.
