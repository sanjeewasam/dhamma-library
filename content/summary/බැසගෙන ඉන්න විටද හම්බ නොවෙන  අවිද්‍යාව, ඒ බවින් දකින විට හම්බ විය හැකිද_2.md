## Sutta referred to
- Directly quoted / referred to, unattributed by title: the dependent-origination sequence *avijjā-paccayā saṅkhārā, saṅkhāra-paccayā viññāṇaṁ, viññāṇa-paccayā nāma-rūpaṁ …*; *jānato passato* / *jānāti–pajānāti–passati* (knowing and seeing); the Buddha's "**what is gained to rejoice at, what lost to grieve over?**"; *sakkāya-diṭṭhi* and *sīlabbata-parāmāsa* as fetters abandoned together; *phassaṁ phassena passati* (seeing contact by contact).
- Referred to, unattributed: a **Zen** teaching that "mountains are mountains, then not mountains, then mountains again"; a **madman** who never knows he is mad; a child's **play-house / play-food**; a **mosquito-slap**.
- Terms used: *avijjā* (ignorance), *phassa* (contact), *saṅkhāra* / *viññāṇa* / *nāma-rūpa*, *upekkhā* / *mūla-upekkhā* (equanimity / root-equanimity), *sammā-diṭṭhi-ñāṇa* (right-view knowledge), *sakkāya-diṭṭhi* (self-view), *sīlabbata-parāmāsa* (clinging to rites and vows), *viññāṇa-agocara* (not within consciousness's range), *nirāyāsa* (effortless), *maññanā* (conceiving). No sutta should be inferred beyond those named.
- **Notes on the source:** the **second segment (Part 2)** of **"Ignorance That Is Not Met Even While Immersed In It — Can It Be Met When Seen As Such?"** — a two-speaker dialogue continuing directly from Part 1. The madman, precepts, and a mosquito-slap are used as illustrations.

## Main dhamma points
- The second segment answering the title directly: ignorance is un-cognizable while one is immersed in it, and even seen "as such" it is not *met* as a thing — only its un-knowability is realized as wisdom, so nothing is gained, nothing lost, and no person "sees and escapes."
- **"Because of contact" understood is useless; contact must be seen by contact.** "Because of contact" is a correct answer, but merely *understanding* "it is all because of contact" is worthless — it lands one back in the mind-object understanding-knot, just as understanding "with formations as condition, consciousness; with consciousness as condition, name-and-form" only grasps the two visible sides while the "because-of" (*paccayā*), the constructing itself, is never caught; one must see contact *by* contact, not set it aside as "because-of."
- **Ignorance cannot be removed, is never met while immersed in it — the madman.** Ignorance is not a thing that exists to be removed; the very "being" we take as real is there through not-knowing, so taking "there is an ignorance to remove" is again taking it as a thing, like "there is a contact." As a madman never knows he is mad and, even cured, never registers "my madness is gone" — never having met his madness, he gained no madness and lost none, and an un-experienced thing cannot be said to have been present or to be gone — so ignorance, while one is immersed in it, is never known, un-cognizable to consciousness, which can therefore never see "formations arise from ignorance." Seen only by an extraordinary wisdom settling at the un-knowable itself, "seeing ignorance" is not a something-seen: going there one finds only an un-understandable nature, and seeing it there is no "I was in ignorance, now it is gone" — nothing gained, nothing lost, which is why the Buddha asks "what is gained to rejoice at, what lost to grieve over?" One cannot even register "wisdom arose," for it is an un-understandable place, and whoever thinks "I have wisdom" holds only a conceiving called "wisdom."
- **No one "sees and escapes"; right view is seeing the view form, not a template.** The person who arises from ignorance-not-known is who fancies "I see nibbāna," but that person was fabricated from not knowing the true nature, so seeing the true nature no "person who was there" is found — only a wrongly-built view, and there is nothing *in* a view to cease; the wrong view simply shows *as* a wrongly-built view, and the capacity to see it so is right-view knowledge, which is not a separate "right view" template to conform to but the seeing of how the "I" is formed. "Is" and "is-not" are real only *with* that fabricated view, real only to the person; the equanimity we abide in cannot even be understood as "equanimity" (the "neither-pleasant-nor-painful middle" we conceive is a comprehended middle, itself the fault), for the real root-equanimity is where "felt" and "not-felt" both fail to register, un-understandable, seen only by a wisdom settling where consciousness cannot — and there no person can arise, no object be grasped, so there is nothing "to do" and nothing "not to do."
- **Both doing and not-doing maintain the person — virtue as self-view's support.** Both doing and not-doing are actions, though the world sees not-doing as mere abstaining; one undertakes "not to kill" precisely because killing is an action to be abstained from, so "not-killing" is itself an action that forms a "not-doer," and the undertaking is exactly where the view forms — *sīlabbata-parāmāsa*, clinging to rites and vows — both keeping and not-keeping maintaining the person and carrying it to a heaven, a brahma-world or a hell. So virtue as ordinarily held maintains personhood, and when *sakkāya-diṭṭhi* is abandoned, *sīlabbata-parāmāsa* falls with it, for once the person is seen as a view "who keeps or does not keep" has no residue; the wisdom-freed one is effortlessly free of killing, neither doing nor not-doing, the noble virtue that guards itself — as not-killing (unlike the reflex mosquito-slap) is itself an effortful action, showing "not-doing" too is a doing. (As in the Zen "mountains again" and the child's play-house, at the last both "me" and "the thing" appear together as just the view.)

## Summary

This second segment answers the title directly. "Because of contact" is a correct answer, but merely *understanding* "it is all because of contact" is worthless, landing one back in the mind-object knot, just as understanding the dependent-origination links only grasps the two visible sides while the constructing itself is never caught; one must see contact *by* contact. Ignorance is not a thing to be removed — the "being" we take as real is there through not-knowing, so "there is an ignorance to remove" is again taking it as a thing.
