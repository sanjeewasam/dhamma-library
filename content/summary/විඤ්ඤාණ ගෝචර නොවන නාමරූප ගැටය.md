## Main dhamma points
- A dialogue holding that name and form are joined only by an impossible act of consciousness — a knot that never happened — so that recognition is a mirage, and only seeing the root not-knowing, itself beyond consciousness's range, undoes it.
- **The lamp lit from a lamp excludes self, other and spontaneous — "not self-made, not other-made."** A lamp lit from another does not receive the flame: its own oil and wick must reach ignition-heat, and the source only lends heat (a support), not a particle of flame passing over — yet without the source no new flame arises. So one can say neither "the flame came from the source lamp," nor "it came from elsewhere," nor "it arose on its own," nor "the source is unneeded"; contact (the "rubbing") makes both cause-side and effect-side arise together. Applied to death, "does the same person go and get reborn?" is like "the fire was in the matchbox and went" — eternalism; "a different person arises" — otherness; "spontaneous" — all three excluded, which is "this puppet is not self-made, nor other-made."
- **Cessation is seeing origination-and-passing together, not "passing = cessation."** In the Satipaṭṭhāna one dwells "contemplating origination, contemplating vanishing, contemplating origination-and-vanishing"; many take "vanishing" for cessation, but were that so the third contemplation would be redundant. Cessation is the knowledge of arising-and-passing seen *together* (*udaya-vaya-ñāṇa*), by which both "when this exists, this is" and "when this is not, this is not" — both being saṁsāra reasoning that grasps causes — are transcended; only in the middle can neither extreme stand. (One layman, better than the monk he studied with, first showed the speaker this gap.)
- **The name-and-form knot never happened; consciousness is the impossible joiner.** A child is taught "blue" by drawing a line joining the word to the colour-patch, but can never draw that line alone, and whoever taught was taught by another, endlessly — mere convention; "blue" does not belong to the colour, nor the line to either, so no name arises from a form nor a form from a name. The knot tying them is a thing that never actually happened, and what shows an impossible task as done is exactly a magic-show — "consciousness is like an illusion," name at one end, form at the other, consciousness in the middle, each the other's condition (the two-reed-bundles, mutual condition). "Here name-and-form wholly cease": cut this knot without remainder and consciousness can find no footing, for name-and-form's standing *is* consciousness's standing.
- **Recognition is a mirage; the mutual knot cannot be caught by consciousness.** A table is never met "as a table"; recognizing is only recognizing, empty of itself, which is why perception is called a mirage — the mirage is given precisely to show recognition's un-recognizability, just as a dog deceived by its reflection has "no dog," yet we who say "no dog" are in the same folly, holding the "it exists" extreme as the animal does. Sound-consciousness arises and we say "there is an ear and a sound"; asked how we know ear and sound, we cite the hearing; asked how we know hearing, we cite ear and sound — mutual, un-falsifiable, and consciousness cannot expose a fabrication it itself wove, like asking the thief to conduct the search. The meeting of three called contact arises only when "thinking" occurs — all things originate from attention, are rooted in contact — yet "thinking" is never itself understood; immersed in conceiving like a fish in water, we cannot grasp it as conceiving, so everything becomes real.
- **Seeing and the "I" are born by one act, so consciousness cannot falsify its own seeing.** When seeing-consciousness arises, "I see" arises with it — the two born of a single act, like the two faces of a spun coin — so if the "I" is born of seeing, it can never find seeing to be false; and only when all four, seen-heard-sensed-cognized, are seen does a view show *as* a view. Arriving home absorbed in another thought, no "I saw" or "I did not see" forms; to prove "I went to town" one can only cite the knotted things seen there, where desire becomes foremost and formations build — but touch the breath and what arises is the mind-base, never the body-base, which is never experienced at all. So the "Ālakārāma/Kāḷakārāma" point stands: one "does not conceive a seer apart from seeing," whereas we, from the first knot ("things exist unseen"), get the other three free — an unseen, a to-be-seen, and a seer — each confirming "there is one who sees."
- **Only seeing the root not-knowing, beyond consciousness's range, undoes it; and this needs a good friend.** The whole wheel is rooted in not-knowing — with ignorance as condition, formations, and even consciousness is so conditioned — so the wrong process stops only when the root, not-knowing, is seen, and then conceiving is realized *as* conceiving. But we abide in the "knowing" existence, consciousness born with every knowing, and never meet a "not-knowing," which is not within consciousness's range — as a fish in water can grasp "water" only by meeting dry land, we cannot see "with not-knowing as condition, formations." Removing self-view is said to be harder than piercing a sevenfold-split horse-hair with another strand; the realization is not in any sutta, which is only a raft and a support (the matchbox still needs rubbing), so a good friend must draw out the dhamma-nature beyond words — which is why "association with a good friend" is ranked first, before "hearing the true teaching," language itself being a distortion of perception in which a distortion's fault cannot be shown. In the end, where seeing can never be understood and, unknotted, one can call it neither "seen" nor "not seen," the very *understanding* of a view or a seeing is itself the suffering.

## Summary

This two-speaker dialogue holds that name and form are joined only by an impossible act. A lamp lit from another does not receive its flame — its own oil and wick reach ignition-heat, the source only lending heat — so one can say neither that the flame came from the source, nor from elsewhere, nor arose on its own, nor that the source is unneeded; contact makes both sides arise together, and "does the same person go and get reborn?" excludes self, other and spontaneous alike: "this puppet is not self-made, nor other-made." In the Satipaṭṭhāna one contemplates origination, vanishing, and origination-and-vanishing together; "vanishing" is not cessation, or the third would be redundant — cessation is seeing arising-and-passing together, transcending both "when this is, this is" and "when this is not, this is not," which are saṁsāra reasoning grasping causes.

The name-and-form knot never happened: a child is taught "blue" by a line joining word to colour, but can never draw it alone, and whoever taught was taught, endlessly — mere convention; "blue" belongs not to the colour, nor the line to either, so no name arises from form nor form from name, and consciousness is the impossible joiner that shows an impossible task as done, "like a magic-show," name at one end, form at the other, each the other's condition. Cut this knot without remainder and consciousness finds no footing, for name-and-form's standing is its standing. Recognition is thus a mirage — a table never met as a table — and we, saying "no dog" of the deceived dog, share its folly, holding "it exists"; sound-consciousness arises and we prove ear-and-sound by hearing and hearing by ear-and-sound, mutual and un-falsifiable, consciousness unable to expose the fabrication it wove. Contact arises only when thinking occurs, all things originating from attention, yet thinking is never itself understood, for immersed in conceiving like a fish in water we cannot grasp it as conceiving, and so all becomes real.

Seeing and the "I" are born by one act, like a coin's two faces, so the "I" born of seeing can never find seeing false; arriving home absorbed, no "I saw" forms, and to prove "I went to town" one cites only the knotted things, where desire leads and formations build, while touching the breath yields the mind-base, never the never-experienced body-base. So one "does not conceive a seer apart from seeing," whereas from the first knot — "things exist unseen" — the unseen, the to-be-seen and the seer follow free. The whole wheel is rooted in not-knowing, and the wrong process stops only when that root, beyond consciousness's range, is seen and conceiving is realized as conceiving; but abiding in "knowing," we never meet a "not-knowing," as a fish grasps water only on dry land. Removing self-view is harder than piercing a sevenfold-split horse-hair, the realization is in no sutta — only a raft, a support that still needs the friend's rubbing — so association with a good friend is ranked first, language being a distortion in which a distortion's fault cannot be shown; and where seeing can never be understood and is neither "seen" nor "not seen," the very understanding of a view is itself the suffering.
