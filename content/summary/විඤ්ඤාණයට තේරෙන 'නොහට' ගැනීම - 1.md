## Main dhamma points
- The first segment on how the awakened one uses formations with wisdom leading — a "non-knotting" awareness in which action is never noted yet nothing is missed — so that nothing new is constructed, though our un-graspable true nature is what we mislabel "unawareness."
- **Consciousness cannot reach where it did not operate; wisdom misses nothing.** A place where consciousness never engaged is forever outside its range, so consciousness can never think about "unawareness"; but the wisdom that sees everywhere does not "miss" things — it is a full awareness in which what consciousness catches and what it cannot catch are alike seen. The object is well recognized, yet the action does not engage or knot with it, for this is a "non-knotting" wisdom.
- **A knot falls only when an action is made real; the arahant notes no action yet is not unmindful.** Only when an action becomes real does a knot fall, and where the action is not understood one cannot recall whether one did it — it happens within awareness but does not go to memory, and that set cannot be re-narrated on demand. This is not unmindfulness: the Buddha asked one who slaughtered cattle "were you mindful?"; the arahant who gave alms in the morning later asks "did you give alms?" — knowing it as a knot, he can still speak of it for use's sake, without the desire-lust that first drove the using, as with a child's play-car where "driving" and "car" are known as mere play.
- **Formations are still used, but with wisdom leading, so nothing new accumulates.** After awakening one still uses formations — words — but with wisdom foremost rather than clinging, so no new formations gather; as a weaver's upper thread still runs (worldly action goes on) while the lower thread no longer falls (formations no longer accumulate), and as watching a film leaves no film real anywhere. This is not dementia or lost memory: the arahant knows exactly how things happened and uses them knowing their manner, but knots nothing new onto saṁsāra, so the world is not freshly rebuilt.
- **Our true nature is un-graspable; we mislabel it "unawareness" against the distortion we built.** The unawareness we point to is really our un-graspable nature-as-it-is, un-thinkable and un-knowable, for we have manufactured a "mindfulness" that is a distortion, and only relative to that distortion do we call the real nature "unawareness"; lacking full mindfulness, we are deceived into calling the distortion "mindfulness." Were we to stop the intermittent thinking — about the breath and the rest — we would simply be in that un-graspable state, within which wisdom nonetheless operates and uses everything.
- **The "un-weavable part" is our real nature; the woven part is the distortion — and to wisdom there is no such division.** We stand divided between a woven, manifest part and a part that cannot be woven at all; the un-weavable is our nature-as-it-is, and the woven part is the distortion built from not knowing it. The questioner's worry — that if the woven part too belongs to the un-weavable, nothing could be remembered and one could not live — is answered: this "cannot-weave" is an inability only to us, whereas to the wisdom that settles there is no "weave / not-weave" division, the weaving being wholly comprehended. As in taking a key from a turtle to lock it and take it again, the actions certainly happen, yet the action itself is never noted; the three actions are within wisdom's range but outside consciousness's, and where noting does not happen, the settled wisdom has nothing un-noted and nothing noted.

## Summary

This first segment turns on how the awakened one uses formations. A place where consciousness never operated is forever outside its range, so consciousness can never think "unawareness," yet the wisdom that sees everywhere misses nothing — what consciousness catches and what it cannot are alike seen, the object well recognized while the action neither engages nor knots, for this is a non-knotting wisdom. A knot falls only when an action is made real, and where action is not understood one cannot recall doing it: it happens within awareness but does not reach memory, and cannot be re-narrated on demand. This is not unmindfulness — the Buddha asked the cattle-slaughterer "were you mindful?", and the arahant who gave alms in the morning later asks "did you give alms?" — for he knows it as a knot and can still speak of it for use, without the desire that first drove the using, as a child knows a play-car is only play.

After awakening one still uses formations, words, but with wisdom foremost rather than clinging, so nothing new accumulates: the weaver's upper thread runs on while the lower no longer falls, and watching a film leaves no film real. It is not dementia; the arahant knows how everything happened and uses it knowing its manner, but knots nothing new onto saṁsāra. What we call "unawareness" is really our un-graspable nature-as-it-is, un-thinkable and un-knowable — we built a "mindfulness" that is a distortion, and only against it do we name the real nature "unawareness," being deceived, for lack of full mindfulness, into calling the distortion mindfulness. We stand divided between a woven, manifest part and a part that cannot be woven; the un-weavable is our real nature, the woven the distortion. The worry that, if the woven too belongs to the un-weavable, nothing could be remembered and one could not live, is met thus: the "cannot-weave" is an inability only to us, while to the settled wisdom there is no weave/not-weave division and the weaving is wholly comprehended — as in taking a key from a turtle to lock and re-take it, the actions happen yet the action is never noted, the three actions lying within wisdom's range but outside consciousness's, so that where noting does not happen the settled wisdom has nothing un-noted and nothing noted.
