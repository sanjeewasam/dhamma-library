## Main dhamma points
- The second segment establishing that the very inability to fabricate becomes knowledge, that "missing" and "blank" arise only because we cannot see the whole, and warning that literalized exaggerations turn the Dhamma itself into Māra.
- **When all weaving is seen as weaving, no formation can be built.** Where wisdom sees these things as un-weavable, no further formations can be constructed — every weaving is seen *as* a weaving, none knotting as "good mindfulness," so no *saṅkhāra* forms; and once the weaving is wholly comprehended there is no separate "un-weavable part," all being seen as weavings and all views as views.
- **Exaggerated tales, taken literally, make the arahant a madman and destroy faith.** The story that an arahant "forgot" who gave him alms that morning is an exaggeration; read literally it makes the arahant a memoryless madman — "who would give alms to memoryless madmen?" — and drains faith, whereas the Buddha addressed Ānanda by name, so the arahant is not memoryless. Such fables push the whole Dhamma toward distortion; reading the canon without realization does this damage, for not every writer wrote from realization, and this is exactly where the Dhamma itself becomes Māra when a right teacher is lacking. The truth is plainer: the arahant does recognize roadside things and people, but having seen the whole as a knot, there is no separate "this part is forgotten," no such boundary.
- **The blank makes the woven part "real"; not seeing origination is what makes things real.** We see only 180° of the circle, and a "blank" comes to us — but to the arahant there is neither blank nor "fill," as one who has booked a train can neither additionally book it nor "not-book" it. The key point made clear: it is precisely *because* of the blank, the un-cognizable gap, that the woven part becomes a hundred-percent real to us; were the whole seen as continuous weaving it could never become real. Seeing origination fully, only cessation is found; not seeing origination is what makes a thing real — so we take the graspable part and watch it "arise and cease," damming a freely-flowing stream, because we cannot see the whole nature as un-weaving. Thus we are caught only in the woven part, and "seeing origination" is itself done only inside the weaving — ninety-nine-point-nine percent of the practice.
- **The three actions are done yet never noted; "missing" appears only because the woven part became real.** As in taking a key from a turtle to lock it and take it again, all the actions certainly happen yet the action itself is never noted — "everything done, yet nothing constructed," seen as "it was not done at all"; we derive "doings" only from the woven part and cannot force-fabricate a "doing" about whether the key was taken or placed. Because the woven part became real, a "missed" piece appears — the blank being the not-seeing of origination — and one then endlessly asks "why did I miss that part?"; but the real question is only the absence of settled wisdom there, a problem that exists nowhere, for we abide within cessation itself, nothing constructed, the un-construction being the true nature.
- **The inability to weave is itself knowledge; the film is seen as film.** Put even "this that I saw and heard" into the un-recallable heap, and one can no longer say "I am, I was, I died" — yet if that itself is wisdom, abiding there is no trouble: with wisdom established, whatever play is played nothing is sewn, for the very inability to weave becomes knowledge. The film is never real anywhere — not that its course is unknown, but the film is seen *as* film, all as weavings, so there is no separate weave/not-weave division; only while seated watching is there a "film," but in the middle we forget it is film and are carried along, flowing in it.

## Summary

This second segment establishes that where wisdom sees these things as un-weavable, no further formations can be built — every weaving seen as a weaving, none knotting, and once the weaving is wholly comprehended there is no separate "un-weavable part." It first clears away a danger: the tale that an arahant "forgot" his morning almsgiver is an exaggeration that, read literally, makes him a memoryless madman and destroys faith — "who would give alms to memoryless madmen?" — whereas the Buddha addressed Ānanda by name; such fables, and reading the canon without realization, push the Dhamma toward distortion and are exactly where it becomes Māra without a right teacher. In truth the arahant recognizes roadside things and people, but having seen the whole as a knot, there is no "this part is forgotten," no such boundary.

The pivot is the "blank": we see only 180°, and a blank comes to us but not to the arahant, for whom there is neither blank nor fill, as one who has booked a train can neither re-book nor un-book it. It is precisely because of that un-cognizable gap that the woven part becomes real to us; seeing origination fully, only cessation is found, and not seeing origination is what makes a thing real — so we take the graspable part, watch it "arise and cease," and dam a freely-flowing stream, caught only in the woven part where "seeing origination" is itself done, ninety-nine-point-nine percent of the practice. As in taking a key from a turtle to lock and re-take it, all the actions happen yet the action is never noted — everything done, nothing constructed, seen as "not done at all" — and we derive "doings" only from the woven part; because it became real, a "missed" piece appears, and one asks endlessly "why did I miss it," when the real lack is only settled wisdom, a problem existing nowhere, since we abide within cessation, nothing constructed. Put even "what I saw and heard" into the un-recallable heap and one can no longer say "I am, I was, I died"; yet if that itself is wisdom, abiding there is no trouble, for the very inability to weave becomes knowledge, and the film — never real anywhere, its course not unknown — is seen as film, all as weavings, with no weave/not-weave division; only while seated is there a film, but in the middle we forget and are carried along.
