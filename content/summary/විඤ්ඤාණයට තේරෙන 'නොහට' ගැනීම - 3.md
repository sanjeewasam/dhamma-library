## Main dhamma points
- The third segment on why "un-construction" is not a blank, why "I didn't see it" is a fabricated extreme, and why the arahant's non-conceiving is misread as forgetfulness — resolving that seeing seen as seeing, and perception as perception, dissolves the whole "missed / remembered" division.
- **"Mindfulness" and "unawareness" are a mutual worldling division; wisdom sees from beyond both.** Where the shop was seen we call it mindfulness, and the other place unawareness — only because we do not know that "other" place do we take this as mindfulness, and taking this as mindfulness makes us call that unawareness; that is the worldling's view, and wisdom must see from a point above both. To the wise the seen shop is seen as being-and-non-being, as un-arisen, and the unseen tree beyond it is equally un-arisen — the same nature, no difference — a seeing that stands just above the mindfulness/unawareness pair, so that no "missed" part is found: the tree passed unseen is also seen, as being-and-non-being.
- **In the un-arisen there is no arising-and-passing to watch either.** The questioner asks: if the arahant "does not conceive," how is arising-and-passing seen there? The base of the question is itself faulty — in the un-arisen, non-conceiving nature there is no arising-and-passing to see, for arising-and-passing, the two constructed extremes, belong only where conceiving happens; so in a place that is un-arisen and non-conceiving, what arises and what passes? Nothing. (The first thought, that "does not conceive" follows from the sheer sharpness of the wisdom, is gently corrected: a conceiving may occur, but it is known as conceiving.)
- **"Un-construction" is not a blank where nothing is recognized.** The practical objection returns — if it were truly "non-construction," things would not be recognized and one would collide with cars, people, a stone on the road. But "non-construction" is not a place where nothing is recognized; recognition does happen (one cannot walk blind), yet there is no "a-thing-recognized" either. Our "non-construction" is only relative to the constructed — a wrong extreme — and when we say of the missed part "I did not see it," we have not seen it with wisdom as impermanent but merely posited "no seeing," the non-existence extreme, knotting a "seeing" and then negating it; not seeing seeing-by-seeing, we fabricate "I did not see."
- **We are trapped among the four perception-terms until neither-perception-nor-non-perception is realized.** Seeing does not own the things seen, yet we knot a "seeing" to a seen thing and call it memory, and everything else falls into the "did not see" heap; so we drop into "no perception" and the rest — na-saññī, no-pi-asaññī and their fellows — a wrong system, and until the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception is realized we are inevitably caught in one of the four: either "recognized," which we name mindfulness, or "not recognized," which we name unawareness. Both are the trap, and the root is not seeing perception as perception; seen as perception, seeing shows clearly as seeing, cleanly separated.
- **The arahant knows seeing as seeing; measuring him by our knot misreads him as memoryless.** The arahant stands at that level — knowing seeing as seeing, with no "did I see this person and not that one," no "oh, I saw you this morning," for that knot is simply absent; we tie the fool's knot and then ask "why does the arahant not remember?" In the alms story he takes the alms and in the evening, reminded, asks "is that so?" — not from anger but from no thought, receiving without a knot of merit-rejoicing, tracking no "the blue-dressed lady who lives in that house," sometimes seeing only at the level of "a human"; the question "is the morning-one the same as now?" is entirely our own thinking. (The teacher notes this seeing-by-seeing point had not before been analysed, and that even the "non-construction" they had discussed was itself grasped from the constructed — the path must ripen there too.)

## Summary

This third segment turns on why "un-construction" is not a blank. Where the shop was seen we call it mindfulness and the other place unawareness — only because we do not know that other place do we take this as mindfulness, and so name that unawareness; that is the worldling's dividing view, whereas wisdom sees from above both, taking the seen shop as being-and-non-being, as un-arisen, and the unseen tree beyond it as equally un-arisen, the same nature, so that no "missed" part is found — the unseen tree is also seen, as being-and-non-being. Hence the questioner's "if the arahant does not conceive, how is arising-and-passing seen?" is faulted at its base: in the un-arisen, non-conceiving nature there is no arising-and-passing, which belongs only where conceiving constructs the two extremes; so nothing arises and nothing passes there.

The practical objection — that "non-construction" would leave things unrecognized and one colliding with cars and stones — is answered: non-construction is not a place where nothing is recognized; recognition happens, yet without "a-thing-recognized," and our "non-construction" is only relative to the constructed, a wrong extreme. When we say of the missed part "I did not see it," we have not seen it as impermanent but posited "no seeing," knotting a seeing and negating it; not seeing seeing-by-seeing, we fabricate "I did not see." Seeing does not own the things seen, yet we knot a seeing to a seen thing, call it memory, and let the rest fall into the "did not see" heap, dropping into "no perception" and its fellows — a wrong system — until the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception is realized, caught meanwhile in one of the four: "recognized," named mindfulness, or "not recognized," named unawareness. The root is not seeing perception as perception; seen so, seeing shows cleanly as seeing. The arahant stands there, knowing seeing as seeing, with no "did I see this one and not that," no "I saw you this morning" — that knot absent — so that measuring him by our fool's knot misreads him as memoryless: in the alms story he asks "is that so?" from no thought, receiving without a merit-knot, tracking no personal details, the "is the morning-one the same as now?" being wholly our thinking.
