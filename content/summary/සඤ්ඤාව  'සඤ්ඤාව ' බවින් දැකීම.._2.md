## Sutta referred to
- Directly quoted in Pāli: the fourfold formula "**na saññasaññī, na visaññasaññī, no pi asaññī, na vibhūtasaññī**" (not one who perceives perception, not one who perceives distortedly, not without perception, not one with perception dissolved) — the description characteristic of the **Kalahavivāda Sutta (Suttanipāta 4.11)** and the arahant level; **not named by title in the talk.** Also "**tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso**" (the meeting of three is contact).
- Referred to, unattributed: *catu-mahā-bhūta* (the four great elements: *paṭhavi–āpo–tejo–vāyo*); *rūpa-kāya adhivacana* / *nāma-dhamma* (name-factors); *cakkhu-viññāṇa* (eye-consciousness); *vedanā–saññā* knot; *pañca-upādāna-skandha* (five clinging-aggregates); *viññāṇa-agocara* (not within consciousness's range). Similes: a mountain seen, licked, smelled, touched and heard; a reflection in the axis of a mirror.
- **Notes on the source:** **Part 2** of **"Seeing Perception as Perception,"** a two-speaker dialogue (a teacher, *mahattaya*, and a questioner) continuing directly from Part 1. It ends cleanly at ~[13:00].

## Main dhamma points
- The second segment closing the fourfold formula on "the inability," and showing that once the mountain is established as a thing it knots with feeling and the sense-bases to birth the seer and the meeting-of-three called contact.
- **All four negations point to the one wisdom settled at "the inability."** Once recognition is realized un-doable, no distorted-recognizer can stand either; "not without perception" refuses "there is no recognition," and "not one with perception dissolved" refuses "recognition does-not-occur," which would slide to "so it occurs." The common factor across all four is the wisdom that settles at the inability — un-reachable by analysis, for only a direct wisdom at the analysis-free nature settles beyond the four, meeting mountain as mountain and water as water with none of the four views: no recognizer, no distorted-recognizer, no "no recognition," no "recognition exists." This is the arahant's level, un-shareable to a person, and consciousness cannot describe it with any four analyses without distorting the very terms.
- **Consciousness analyses only what it is caught in; at its level only "cannot-understand" is realized.** We analyse only by taking "chair" or "mountain" as given — assuming "perception recognized a mountain" — and even "there is no mountain" first posits a mountain and negates it, sliding from existence to non-existence; so at the consciousness-level none of the four fits, and only the "cannot-understand" is realized: a mountain cannot be understood from "mountain," name is seen as name and form as form. Where the knot is loosened consciousness can find no footing — if name is loosened as name and form seen as form, the knot is realized as never having fallen — so consciousness can stand neither on the mountain nor on the shape-colour-earth perceptions, which then cannot knot to the "seen / not-seen" view nor to feeling.
- **Established as a thing, the mountain knots with feeling and the sense-bases, birthing the seer and contact.** If "mountain" is established it knots with feeling — it can be seen, licked, smelled, touched, and heard when rock falls — for the un-doable recognition, not known as un-doable, becomes a thing "met" through hearing, seeing, sensing and knowing, and knots to the feelings; this very knotting of feeling and perception is the five clinging-aggregates, the meeting-of-three called contact. The mountain met by the eye, form knotted to eye, gives "I see the mountain," and with that seeing-view the seer is created — not before-and-after but simultaneously, from not knowing the wrong recognition; eye and form are knotted by the "because" of eye-consciousness, the meeting of three is contact right there, and from that knot the reflection-in-the-axis, the view "seeing," arises.

## Summary

This second segment closes the fourfold formula. Once recognition is realized un-doable, no distorted-recognizer can stand; "not without perception" refuses "there is no recognition," and "not with perception dissolved" refuses "recognition does-not-occur," which would slide to "so it occurs" — so all four point to the one wisdom settled at the inability, un-reachable by analysis, for only a direct wisdom at the analysis-free nature settles beyond the four, meeting mountain as mountain with none of the four views. This is the arahant's level, and consciousness cannot render it in any four analyses without distorting the terms, since it analyses only what it is caught in: it takes "mountain" as given, and even "there is no mountain" first posits one and negates it. So at the consciousness-level only the "cannot-understand" is realized — a mountain cannot be understood from "mountain," name seen as name, form as form — and where the knot is loosened consciousness finds no footing, standing neither on the mountain nor on the shape-colour-earth perceptions, which then cannot knot to the "seen / not-seen" view nor to feeling.

But if "mountain" is established it knots with feeling — seen, licked, smelled, touched, heard when rock falls — for the un-doable recognition, not known as un-doable, becomes a thing met through hearing, seeing, sensing and knowing, knotting to the feelings; this knotting of feeling and perception is the five clinging-aggregates, the meeting-of-three called contact. The mountain met by the eye, form knotted to eye, gives "I see the mountain," and with that seeing-view the seer is created — not before-and-after but simultaneously, from not knowing the wrong recognition — eye and form knotted by the "because" of eye-consciousness, the meeting of three being contact right there, and from that knot the reflection-in-the-axis, the view "seeing," arises.
