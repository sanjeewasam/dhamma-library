## Sutta referred to
- Directly quoted in Pāli: "**pacchuppannañca yo dhammaṁ tattha tattha vipassati**" (whatever is the present phenomenon, one sees insight into it right there — the **Bhaddekaratta Gāthā, MN 131**), glossed as "see the present" = "see arising-by-condition" = "see the non-arising"; "**antojaṭā bahijaṭā, jaṭāya jaṭitā pajā**" (a tangle within, a tangle without, humanity entangled in a tangle — the **Jaṭā Sutta, SN 7.6**); "**nāma-rūpa-paccayā viññāṇaṁ, viññāṇa-paccayā nāma-rūpaṁ**" (name-and-form conditions consciousness, and consciousness name-and-form); the term **yathābhūta-ñāṇa-dassana** (knowledge-and-vision of things as they are); **mano-sañcetanā āhāra** (the nutriment of mental volition).

## Main dhamma points
- A dialogue using the dream-and-waking (live / non-live) comparison to break self-view, showing that both are equally "seen," that the "because" of contact dissolves under its own logic, and that seeing this built structure at the very moment is knowledge-and-vision of things as they are.
- **See the present = see the non-arising; the made thing must not be examined for its fault.** "See the present right there" means see arising-by-condition, which is to see the non-arising: seeing the condition rightly, one finds the "condition" is never met in this moment. We instead examine the made thing — the glass, its colour, shape, earth-element — for a fault, whereas the "making" is un-met by mind; even describing the glass by name-factors (name-body designation) and "seeing origination" that way merely swaps one demon for another, changing the suit, for the very condition was built by not-knowing.
- **The glass and the seeing mutually condition, with no root and no end — which is the transcending.** "The glass exists because seen; seeing exists because the glass exists" is mutual and un-resolvable, like hen and egg, so this conditioned thing has no root and no end — saṁsāra without discoverable beginning — and seeing no root *is* the end, for a beginningless thing needs none put to it; this seeing is the transcending. The "because" is contact, and the one who argues from it is himself the argument, the meeting-of-three; so it cannot be settled by logic — it is beyond reasoning, since one's own argument disproves oneself and leaves no answer.
- **The dream is as fully "live" as the waking; neither is the real one.** In a dream one sees the very people one was talking with; there too "these people exist" is fully evident, the glass seen cannot be denied, nor the seeing, and a chasing dog brings real fear and, if it bites, real pain and weeping — no evidence that the dream is not live, so at that moment it is live; and this present moment is live too. Which then is the real live? Neither. We call the dream false only because *this* is real, but the dream is equally real, and if both are live both are real; then "false" drops out, and it can no longer serve as the condition to make this "real," nor can this even be called "real," which needs a false to contrast. No lie can be found to prove either real. As all-identical trees or a single tree allow no "tall / short," so when the two become equal "live / non-live" cannot stand, needing two; with no two, no mind can arise and no mental-volition food can form — the mind ceases there, and the Buddha teaches no further, this being knowledge-and-vision of things as they are.
- **The tangle of knots, and the error of "the mind fabricated it."** The dream-dog was no happened-thing, and is not now; a self-made fabrication, "the non-happened" — but is it "the mind fabricates because not-knowing," or "a mind was formed"? Both fell together: the knot fell and the mind formed. One must not rest at "a mind existed and made it up," the heart-made dog, for that very division is another tangle — a tangle within (mind with name-and-form) and a tangle without, then a third knot joining them, "the outer is what is seen from within." Loosen one knot — the inner, the outer, or the dream — and this side finds no footing, for a resolved knot can birth no mind; the mind's food is intention, formed from wrong view, and where the view is straightened no two remain to feed it.
- **Contact, examined, dissolves under its own logic; self-view breaks.** The dream is falsified only by "there was no contact there" — no eye, no form, no ear, no sound — so "this is live because contact exists." But the dream too has seeing, and seeing requires an eye and a form, so the dream must have contact too and be equally live; thus the very "contact exists here" that made this real, examined, collapses — for seeing needed no real eye and form there, so seeing is only seeing, with no eye-form contact, no true and no false, and no "me," and self-view breaks. This "contact exists" is the primal ignorance to be seen through; name-and-form conditions consciousness and consciousness name-and-form, both seen as they are with no true or false, the "seer" himself only a piece of name-form and consciousness, so no "I" is found. It cannot be proved by logic but must be self-realized, by examining in one's own life how it is built, for seeing the building at this very moment is the breaking.

## Summary
