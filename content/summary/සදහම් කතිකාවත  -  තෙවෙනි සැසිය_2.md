## Sutta referred to
- Directly referred to in Pāli: "**cakkhuñca paṭicca rūpe ca uppajjati cakkhu-viññāṇaṁ**" (dependent on eye and forms, eye-consciousness arises); "**tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso**" (the meeting of three is contact); the Buddha's "**I neither strove nor stood still, and so crossed the flood**" (the **Oghataraṇa Sutta, SN 1.1**); the term **viññāṇañcāyatana** (base of infinite consciousness), reached by one who keeps finding "a mind, a mind, a mind."
- Referred to, unattributed: the path as **nirāyāsa** (effortless) set beside the instruction to do **yoniso-manasikāra** (wise attention), noted as seemingly contradictory; *viññāṇa-pariññā* (full comprehension of consciousness) as *paññā*; *nāma-rūpa*, *diṭṭha-suta-muta-viññāta*, *maññanā*, *bhava* (existence), *śāśvata / nitya* (eternalism), *vacī-saṅkhāra* (verbal formation), *kammaṭṭhāna* (meditation subject). Similes: a knot tied in a string; a hen and egg; a small child who has not yet learned names; the rotation of the earth; a fan seen.
- **Notes on the source:** **Part 2** of **"Dhamma Discussion — Third Session,"** a group dialogue (a teacher, a questioner, and a lady, "Anti"). **Transcription artifacts (flagged):** the memory-and-forgetting passage is **partly duplicated** (lines ~199–224 recur at ~241–266); and the segment **ends mid-sentence** ("if one abides in it, it can never…") at line ~297 — read as a cutoff.

## Main dhamma points
- The second segment showing that wisdom sees consciousness arise from its own knot, that the knot belongs to neither end, that "become a child" is a false shortcut, and that we abide in a memory, a seeing, a hearing that can never actually be experienced.
- **Wisdom sees the mind arise from its root; it is not a separate watcher.** There must be a difference, two things, to think at all, so the mind arises by condition, and "seeing the condition" means that not seeing the condition is precisely what births consciousness — not that a mind pre-exists and thinks. Seeing the root of the mind's arising, all "mind-made" stories are seen as fairy-tales; whereas one who merely notes "that too is mind-made, a thought" endlessly finds "a mind, a mind" and drifts to the base of infinite consciousness, a piece always left. Right view sees even the mind arise, and this full comprehension of consciousness is wisdom — not a separate wisdom watching, but a seeing from the very knot that transcends it, for the Buddha points to no separate cessation elsewhere.
- **The knot belongs to neither end; loosen the ends and the middle does not remain.** Consciousness ties the knot grasping the two, live and non-live, and not seeing "seeing as seeing" makes the comparison of one false and one true; but the knot belongs to neither end, tied from a place belonging to neither, as a knot in a string is at neither end. We grasp the two ends — eye and form — never the knot, and can find no fault in mutually-conditioned ends, like hen and egg; loosen the two ends, seen as name-and-form so that "no eye-and-form was met," and the middle — the "seeing," the contact — does not remain either but vanishes. Seeing that there is *no* middle is the point, not "seeing from the middle."
- **"Seeing exists" is the worldling's un-seen immersion; the mind forms from not-knowing, not by a hand.** First comes seeing, then the eye-and-form knot, and one cannot choose not to knot, for eye-consciousness arises grasping-and-knotting the two. Looking at a fan it is directly "fan," a name, no "seeing" concept arising — "seeing" is never understood, so we leap to "there is a seeing in the mind connecting eye and form," and holding "it exists" is exactly the worldling's not-seeing the four views he is immersed in, being himself the reality-status of seen-heard-sensed-cognized, whose fault cannot be found. Because "seeing exists" the fan cannot be denied, nor "an organ that sees"; but seeing must arise *from* this supposed existing thing — a mind forming from not-knowing, not fabricated by a hand — and one must take care, for if caught in "the mind fabricates," a permanent mind returns.
- **Escape is a happening, not a doing; "become a child" stops at raw folly.** All this is un-doable from the start; verbally one says "discipline seeing by seeing," but disciplining is something that *happens*, not something done, and language's fault is that it reads like a doing — so the path is called effortless even while "do wise attention" is said, and the Buddha "neither strove nor stood still and crossed." A small child sees, but it is no "fan" to him, only eye-and-form with no data; yet the child is not pure — he has a seeing-existence and a hearing-existence, turning to sounds and reaching for toys, baiting hooks in curiosity to know the seen — so "become like a child" merely halts at raw folly (and a newborn who died would then see nibbāna, which is false). One must instead see the *root* of seeing and hearing, the un-knowable place whence consciousness knots the two, whereupon the whole game of consciousness appears, the knot can no longer be tied, conceiving wears away, and "the hearing itself is suffering" shows.
- **We abide in a memory, a seeing, a hearing that can never be experienced.** "Fan" is an un-recognized thing named from conceiving, from memory — but one holds no memory; "memory" is worldly convention. We think "Anti forgot something," yet a forgotten thing is never met at "forgetting"; when it "comes back" we call it remembering and retroject "it was forgotten before," as with sleep — so it was never "in memory" anywhere, and the forgotten was never met, "forgotten" being a this-moment construct. Thus we abide in a memory, a seeing, a hearing — the eternalist permanence — and if one truly abides in a memory one cannot "remember" anew, in hearing cannot "hear" anew, in seeing cannot "see" anew; wisdom settles at that inability and the "I-can" conceiving shows as a conceiving. As the earth's rotation is never experienced standing on it, believed only on report, and cannot be seen either from the earth or from space, so our seeing and hearing, abided *in*, can never be experienced. (One must therefore drop even the last clinging, still the verbal formation, and grasp a single meditation-subject rather than discuss for hours.)

## Summary

This second segment shows wisdom seeing the mind arise from its own root. Since thinking needs a difference, two things, the mind arises by condition, and "seeing the condition" means that not seeing it is what births consciousness — not that a mind pre-exists and thinks; seeing the root, all "mind-made" stories are fairy-tales, whereas merely noting "that too is a thought" drifts endlessly to the base of infinite consciousness. Full comprehension of consciousness is wisdom itself, not a separate watcher but a seeing from the very knot that transcends it. The knot belongs to neither end — as a knot in a string is at neither — tied from a place belonging to neither; we grasp the two ends, eye and form, never the knot, and find no fault in mutually-conditioned ends like hen and egg; loosen the ends, seen as name-and-form, and the middle, the "seeing," does not remain, so that seeing *no* middle is the point.

"Seeing exists" is the worldling's un-seen immersion: first comes seeing, then the eye-form knot one cannot choose not to tie, for eye-consciousness arises knotting the two; a fan is directly "fan," "seeing" never understood, so we leap to "a seeing in the mind connects eye and form," and being the reality-status of seen-heard-sensed-cognized we cannot find its fault, unable to deny the fan or the seeing-organ. But seeing arises from this supposed thing — a mind forming from not-knowing, not by a hand — and if caught in "the mind fabricates," a permanent mind returns. Escape is a happening, not a doing: "discipline seeing by seeing" reads as a doing only by language's fault, the path being effortless even as "wise attention" is enjoined, the Buddha neither striving nor standing still. A child sees but has no "fan," only eye-and-form — yet he has a seeing- and hearing-existence, baiting curiosity's hooks, so "become a child" halts at folly; one must see the *root* of seeing and hearing, the un-knowable place whence consciousness knots the two, whereupon the whole game appears, the knot cannot be tied, conceiving wears away, and "hearing itself is suffering" shows. And we abide in a memory, a seeing, a hearing that can never be experienced: a forgotten thing is never met at forgetting, "remembering" retrojects "it was forgotten," so it was never in memory — and if one truly abides in a memory one cannot remember anew, in hearing cannot hear anew; wisdom settles at that inability and "I-can" shows as a conceiving. As the earth's rotation is never experienced standing on it and cannot be seen from earth or space, so our seeing and hearing, abided in, can never be experienced.
