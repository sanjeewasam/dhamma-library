## Main dhamma points
- The short close resolving that "seeing and I" can be stated neither as two nor as one, so seeing is a view, not a thing — a bondage that, seen as a knot, can be undone, whereas self-view is otherwise never removed.
- **"Seeing and I" cannot be understood as two, as one, or separately.** They are not two and not separate: to understand "the view, seeing, is me" is wrong, and to understand "seeing is not me" is also wrong; one cannot speak of seeing apart from "me," nor separate the seeing to speak of "me," nor remove "me" to speak of seeing — so neither stands, and these are not things one can state at all, which is precisely what "it cannot be understood" means.
- **Seeing is a view, not a thing — a bondage that can be undone only when seen as a knot.** To take "there is a seeing," "a thing exists," is just a bondage; and because it is a bondage it can be removed — were it a real existing thing, it could not. If the view is corrected, it is only a knotted thing with nothing to be done, and seeing it *as* a knot is what lets it be removed; otherwise the "I"-kind, self-identity, can never be removed.

## Summary
