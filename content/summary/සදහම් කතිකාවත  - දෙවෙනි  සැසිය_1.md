## Sutta referred to
- Directly referred to, unattributed by title: the **five-aggregate similes** — form like a **foam-lump**, feeling like a **water-bubble**, perception like a **mirage**, formations like a **banana-trunk** (peeled, no heartwood), consciousness like an **illusion / magician** (the **Pheṇapiṇḍūpama Sutta, SN 22.95**), with the **magician at the four-way crossroads**; "**sabbe saṅkhārā aniccā … yadā paññāya passati**" (all formations are impermanent — when one sees with wisdom, **Dhammapada 277**); the four knowledges **dukkhe ñāṇaṁ, samudaye ñāṇaṁ, nirodhe ñāṇaṁ, magge ñāṇaṁ**; "**viññāṇaṁ anidassanaṁ**" (consciousness non-manifest) and *appatiṭṭhita* (unestablished) consciousness; "**cakkhuñca paṭicca rūpe ca uppajjati cakkhu-viññāṇaṁ**"; "**tiṇṇaṁ saṅgati phasso**"; the **Dhamma as a raft** (*kullūpama*, MN 22); the **eighteen elements** (*aṭṭhārasa dhātu*).
- Referred to, unattributed: *avijjā / mūla-moha* (ignorance / root-delusion), *sakkāya-diṭṭhi*, *bhava* (existence), *maññanā*, the four great elements and the scientific "electron–proton–quantum" refinement. A pit on the road.

## Main dhamma points
- The second session pressing that origination cannot be understood by consciousness, that grasping the met thing only rebuilds "I," and that the impermanence of a thing can never be seen — even the eighteen elements and the Dhamma being copies within the distortion, released like a raft.
- **Watching origination by grabbing a thing is the very error; it is knowledge, not consciousness.** The five aggregates are shown as foam, bubble, mirage, banana-trunk and illusion — all made to appear as existing, consciousness the illusion that so shows them — and watching origination this way satisfies the mind; but the question "is there a better way to watch origination?" has no answer because the prior fault is uncaught: watching a thing's origination by grabbing the thing. It cannot be worded, so one who says "it cannot be worded" speaks uselessly, and "origination" as anyone teaches it is only this heard bit — which is why cessation is never realized. Origination is not understandable, hence it is called *knowledge* — knowledge of suffering, origination, cessation, path — for were consciousness able to grasp it, the Buddha would not have called it knowledge, and "all formations are impermanent" must be seen "with wisdom," which would be needless if consciousness sufficed; what must be seen is the non-happening.
- **The met thing is grabbed only because impermanence is unseen, and with it "I" arises.** Impermanence unseen, a thing seems to exist; not seeing that "the very meeting of a thing is impermanent" is why a thing seems met, and — the person-status covering it — from the met thing "I" arise, as in a dream chasing a dog. Without wisdom we grab the met thing, and grabbing it "I exist" registers with it, so there is a thing to examine and a "me"; then any conclusion — "chair is a concept, a dream, false, any label" — but who labels? "I" arise with each label, as in a dream, grabbing the not-known-built thing, abiding in a view built from it, viewing its fault from that view. Right view can never do this, for the view formed from a thing met, which arose from not seeing the root-delusion — whose not-seeing is why consciousness arises and names it a "thing."
- **The meeting-of-three sets everything real, so the fault of "understanding" never registers.** Form met with consciousness, "chair" met with knowing, consciousness real, chair real, met by an eye also real — dependent on eye and forms, eye-consciousness arises — and once this story forms it *is* our view: the eye "mine," form "mine," sound "mine," feeling "mine," all set, so the fault of understanding never registers, because "I" arise through this very understanding, my basis and my existence riding with it. However finely the understood thing is broken — four elements, then electron-proton, then "just energy," science's "advance" being the making-real of a folly-born thing — the "I" arising with each breaking cannot be stopped, so the meeting-of-three is not understandable; taking it as understood-and-written is why its fault cannot be found, and consciousness re-thinks "the eye is impermanent" and "consciousness an illusion," feels freed, and is caught again. No breaking ever removes self-view, for understanding builds on understanding, the eye un-falsifiable, "how does this appear without an eye?", and self-view builds to the exact measure of the view — the four-way-crossroads magician, un-graspable from any side.
- **Seeing and hearing are the un-falsifiable "I"; that is the whole trap.** However false the glass is proven, while I have seeing I cannot escape, for seeing built, "I" am seeing's reflection, my "I exist" never falsified — which is why I argue — until I see seeing *as* a view; reversed, "seeing is a conceiving" cannot be said, since I know the glass exists only because seen, and know I am here only because seen, so "I am awake" is these very understandings, "I see this, I hear this," and it never registers that seeing and hearing are a wrong understanding at the very root, on which my birth stands and toward which all else is pulled. Through this view "I" arise, so while the hearing-view is me I can never find heard things false, and while seeing is me I can never find seen things delusion — my very evidence of "I exist" being this existence of form, sound, seeing, sensing and cognizing, where "I" arise; and we, the born "I," then try to break these down, which never ends.
- **The eighteen elements and the Dhamma are copies within the distortion, released like a raft.** The eighteen elements — six bases, six objects, six attentions — take attention "from the mind," but the mind too is called an illusion, so this, seen by that illusion, is illusion; the eighteen-element teaching arose not because eighteen elements exist but because this distortion formed, shown to reveal, at some level, the nature of the built thing that became real, "there is an eye, a form, an ear, a sound," since its fault cannot be told at once to one drowning in folly. One who makes the eighteen elements real and searches returns to the demon, re-making consciousness real, for to whom do the eighteen elements register but "me" again; so the eighteen-element teaching must itself be seen as one more thing born of the fault of understanding, not a single piece real, the raft released rather than kept with a few pieces. Even "Dhamma" is a copy within the distortion, "this Dhamma I speak," which is why it must be released — given only to escape the fallen trap, a raft made by another, and "the meeting-of-three exists" is said as "there is a pit on the road": to leap in, or to avoid it? So caught in it, it cannot be understood or falsified, and without this insight "is the eye permanent or impermanent?" is dreaming of a place unvisited — for the eye's impermanence cannot be seen by grabbing the held eye, registering only to one who saw impermanence, and only to whom does "the eye's very birth is suffering" show, the labels "impermanent, false, illusion, dream" being given only to the met thing, as to a child.

## Summary

This second session presses that origination cannot be understood by consciousness. The five aggregates are shown as foam, bubble, mirage, banana-trunk and illusion, and watching origination this way satisfies the mind, but "is there a better way?" has no answer because the prior fault is uncaught: watching a thing's origination by grabbing the thing. It cannot be worded, and "origination" as taught is only a heard bit, which is why cessation is not realized; it is called *knowledge*, not consciousness — for were consciousness able, the Buddha would not have called it knowledge, nor said formations must be seen "with wisdom." Impermanence unseen, a thing seems to exist, and from the met thing "I" arise as in a dream chasing a dog; grabbing it, "I exist" registers with it, and any conclusion — "chair is a concept, false, any label" — has an "I" arising with each label, viewing the built thing's fault from a view built from it, which right view can never do, since the view formed from a thing met, arisen from not seeing the root-delusion.

The meeting-of-three sets all real: form met with consciousness, chair real, eye real, "dependent on eye and forms, eye-consciousness arises," and this story *is* our view, so the fault of understanding never registers, "I" arising through the very understanding on which my existence rides; however finely the thing is broken — four elements, electron-proton, "energy" — the "I" arising with each breaking cannot be stopped, so the meeting-of-three is not understandable, and consciousness re-thinks "the eye is impermanent, consciousness an illusion," feels freed, and is caught again — the four-way-crossroads magician, un-graspable from any side. Seeing and hearing are the un-falsifiable "I": however false the glass is proven, while I have seeing I cannot escape, my "I exist" never falsified until seeing is seen as a view, for "I am awake" is these very understandings, a wrong understanding at the root on which my birth stands. Finally the eighteen elements and the Dhamma are copies within the distortion — the eighteen-element teaching arising because the distortion formed, not because they exist, and even "Dhamma" a copy — so they must be released like a raft, "the meeting-of-three exists" said as "there is a pit on the road," to be avoided, not leapt into; and without this insight "is the eye permanent or impermanent?" is dreaming of an unvisited place, for the eye's impermanence cannot be seen by grabbing the held eye, and only to one who saw impermanence does "the eye's very birth is suffering" show, the labels given only to the met thing, as to a child.
