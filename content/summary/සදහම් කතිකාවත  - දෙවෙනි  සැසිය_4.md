## Main dhamma points
- The closing segment on how wisdom settles at the inability where neither doing nor not-doing can be done, why "action" is never understood, and why realization is un-doable — reached only by a good friend, never by understanding, which is itself the harm.
- **Wisdom stands in neither doing nor not-doing; the noble virtue dissolves the person.** One can neither do demerit nor merit — by nature impossible — for wisdom is the knowing settled at the inability where both actions cannot be done; both doing and not-doing are effort, and to one who sees both as effort comes "neither standing still nor striving, cross without associating the two." There both the "abiding" and the "striving" are "me," which is self-view — merit and demerit done by "me," to send "me" to a good or bad place, so "I guard myself," which cannot be forced but comes only by realization. The virtue there is the noble virtue dear to the noble ones, differing from our precepts that send the person to a good place: it is the wisdom that *dissolves* the person, called "virtue" but really self-guarded by nature; so, both un-done, one is "abandoned of merit and demerit," able neither to keep precepts nor to break them, for keeping means "I keep," possible only with a self, and intention being what is called kamma, seeing how intention arises, wisdom settles in that un-arising nature.
- **By nature we abide in the extinguished; the "do / not-do" story covers it.** It was not said "we should abstain from demerit"; it was shown by nature that there is a "can't-do," not a "can-do" — and in an inability both "I do" and "I must not-do" are folly, as where breath and feeling are not understood both "I feel" and "I do not feel" are folly. So by nature we are truly in the right place, abiding in the extinguished, and not seeing it we are caught in the "do / not-do" story, hunting things to do, the doing itself covering this nature; and one who sees that nature cannot be forced to the four woeful states, for no cause and no condition form there, so no consciousness arises, no view, no "I keep or do not keep precepts," no self-run system, no saṁsāra built — an unshakable place, virtue self-guarded, un-broken by any object, since an object-free nature cannot be broken by an object.
- **"Action" is never understood, for the un-cognizable place has no condition.** It sounds easy to look at, but if one cannot bring that "stopped nature" to mind and can do nothing by one's control, it is not an action — for an "action" is understood only as "I do something." Lying in bed the limbs turn and stretch with no concern, not even understood, and that un-understood nature is not noted, no copy strikes, consciousness cannot grasp it — such a nature, the very inability. "Action" is only convention: "bathing," thought of as separate acts, is never understood as "bathing," and cannot be shown, for asked to show it one shows the body, the water, the shower — those, the conditions consciousness uses, knotting them to cover the inability — while "bathing" itself, the contact, is never understood, having no condition; so "where do you go?" is met with a bag-of-coconuts answer — "my leg moves, the ground is there" — talk of leg and ground for "where do you go," which is madness.
- **Realization is un-doable, reached only through a good friend; understanding itself is the harm.** The nature is like piercing a sevenfold-split horse-hair — one cannot even split a hair sevenfold, let alone pierce it — so it is un-doable by thought or trial, and removing self-view is said harder still, a knowledge so powerful it settles only by nature, un-givable even by a Buddha, or the Buddha would have shown all beings nibbāna before his parinibbāna. Since even a Buddha could not, one realizes it only by rousing one's own effort, by some prior merit, meeting a good friend and hearing and seeing — no one points it out with a finger, and it cannot be understood-and-done, but must be led by having the fault in each thing said tapped out, "there, that is wrong," "this way it cannot go," a journey no video can complete, needing to stop along this line and sharpen the blade weekly as it goes blunt. And the fertilizer given round about must never be cut off — yet pouring every fertilizer kills the seedling, every company's "fertilizer" making the mind understand facts nicely, and that very understanding is the harm.

## Summary
