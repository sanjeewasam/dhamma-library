## Main dhamma points
- A distilled statement that watching origination by grabbing the already-become thing only *understands* the inability instead of *realizing* it, so cessation is never reached; and that only where non-becoming is seen does becoming appear rightly, the Four Truths arising in one instant.
- **Watching origination on the "become thing" only understands the inability; it is never realized.** We take the already-become effect and watch origination after the becoming — as one needs no re-teaching of the in-and-out breath once it is felt — and the nature is that we were "without concern," breath felt or not, that unconcern being an equanimity which is the un-knowability itself; not knowing it is an inability is why we "know," so the fault is that we go to watch the inability *grabbing the very known thing*. From the known thing a definition of the un-knowable is fitted: "if the past had not arisen, the future could not," so we understand the inability by a fact — and thus the inability is not realized but only understood, understood through the become-thing's absence-side, after which the re-arising conditions for consciousness are already made. It does not register as a fault but seems correct and unshakable, so from a become-origination one can never reach cessation by grabbing the become; if one could, the many who watch origination this way would all have seen cessation.
- **Only one who sees non-becoming sees becoming rightly; the true absence cannot be spoken.** "Becoming" is the action and "the become" is its fruit, so to see becoming rightly one must see non-becoming rightly — cessation being arising-and-passing seen together, to which "becoming" appears from its root, not as a "become thing." In the demonstration, "a non-feeling never met is met this very moment" registers specially, so the true absence-extreme, where it strikes, can no longer be spoken as "an absence within a non-feeling" — for all this while, by "no non-feeling," we posited the present moment as a "feeling," and seeing that non-feeling too arises right here, one can no more speak an "absence" in it than keep fire and water placed together; no going to origination follows, and that itself is impermanence, by which the "becoming" wears away bit by bit.
- **Seen together, origination and cessation settle the Four Truths in one instant, effortlessly.** We watch cessation from a become-origination, whereas rightly seeing origination only cessation appears, and because cessation appears origination appears rightly — not about a thing — so where non-becoming registers, "becoming" appears, and the Four Truths' knowledge arises each in a single instant; thus watching origination as usually described, over four years, one does not get cessation at the fourth like a degree. Only where cessation is rightly seen can one fearlessly say "I saw origination," and only to one who sees origination and cessation together does "the very constructing is suffering" show — the constructing of a feeling itself suffering, a non-met thing appearing met, so formations-suffering registers, and the three seen in one moment is "the path develops," not a separate path a person develops; the "person" cannot arise this moment, the feeling being empty of feeling, feeling itself silenced and no "absence in a non-feeling" speakable again.

## Summary

This compact dialogue explains why cessation is not realized however much one watches origination. We take the already-become effect and watch origination after the becoming — as one needs no re-teaching of the breath once it is felt — the nature being that we abide "without concern," which is the un-knowability itself, and not knowing it is an inability is why we "know"; so the fault is watching the inability *while grabbing the known thing*, from which a definition of the un-knowable is fitted — "if the past had not arisen, the future could not" — so the inability is not realized but only understood, through the become-thing's absence-side, after which the re-arising conditions for consciousness are already made. It seems correct and unshakable, so from a become-origination one can never reach cessation; if one could, all who watch it thus would have seen cessation.

"Becoming" is the action and "the become" its fruit, so to see becoming rightly one must see non-becoming — cessation being arising-and-passing seen together, to which becoming appears from its root, not as a become thing.
