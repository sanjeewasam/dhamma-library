Similes: the H2O regress (water → H2O → molecules → atoms → electrons/protons, endlessly a fruit and never a cause); a mirage-mound (*marīci*); a dog barking in a dream, un-locatable as to where it was born, came from or went.
- **Notes on the source:** **Part 2** of **"Nibbāna That Is Neither Saṁsāra Nor Apart From Saṁsāra."** It begins mid-flow, continuing directly from Part 1 (opening "we should understand in that way"), and is a single continuous recording that closes cleanly on the dream simile.

## Main dhamma points
- The second part holding that seeing the distortion as distortion is right view, that a formed thing can never yield origination (the H2O regress), and that one freed by this view is freed from both saṁsāra and nibbāna — for nibbāna is neither apart from saṁsāra nor saṁsāra itself.
- **Seeing the distortion as distortion is right view; nothing remains and nothing ceases.** These three — impermanent, suffering, not-self — are what the "permanent, pleasant, self" we have taken up really is, held so only from not knowing it is a distortion; and seeing that distortion as a distortion, that very seeing is the natural state (*prakṛti*), the clear right view (*sammā-diṭṭhi*). When the view is right, there is at the end nothing that remained and nothing that ceased — not a pleasure that became suffering, not a self that became not-self, not even a "dying," for "I existed and I die" comes only from "permanent-pleasant-self" and "I persist"; existence being built from a non-persistence, when the non-persistence is seen as non-persistence there is no talk of existence or of non-existence. Purifying this same view up to arahatship is nothing new — only the view sharpening, growing keen enough to see all views form, the wisdom settling so that every arising word and consciousness is seen exactly as it forms.
- **A formed thing can never yield origination — the H2O regress.** Asked what water is, one says H2O; asked what hydrogen and oxygen are, the molecules; asked the molecules, further still — so we think we found water's cause, H2O, yet in time H2O too cannot "just be" and needs a cause, and hunting that cause a fresh cause appears while the old becomes a fruit, endlessly, so that looking back there is only fruit upon fruit. From a formed thing origination can never be seen — every direction yields only a fruit — which is the depth of the Buddha's word that cessation is realized only where origination is seen: watching origination this way we never realize cessation, for H2O is no origination, no cessation, no cause, no wise attention, only another fruit met through a further cause.
- **The root of not-knowing never falls to the mind; until it does, both saṁsāra and nibbāna "exist."** We never find why the not-knowing is, for the not-knowing's cause is never caught — the root of dependent-origination never falls to the mind — and as long as it does not, until ignorance is seen as ignorance by ignorance itself, "cessation is realized" can never happen; and just so long there both a saṁsāra and an extinguishing "exist." For we look only from the formed: watching from a thing that has originated, only a fruit is ever met, and we fit the fruit to a cause.
- **One freed by this view is freed from both saṁsāra and nibbāna.** Seeing thus, one is freed from both saṁsāra and nibbāna — there is no separate saṁsāra to be met, no separate nibbāna escaping it, the very seeing of the distortedness of the "saṁsāra" that formed being nibbāna; so there is no nibbāna apart from saṁsāra and no saṁsāra apart from nibbāna, and one can no more show an extinguishing apart from origination than a cessation apart from an arising. We suppose we have "attained" a nibbāna, but we speak of nibbāna only because of saṁsāra, and since the meeting-of-three has solidified a permanence we hold "the existing thing was lost" and imagine a separate nibbāna free of it — not seeing that "the very sense that there is something" arose from not-knowing; seeing not-knowing by not-knowing, the root that builds both "it exists" and "it is lost" appears, both extremes seen at once. Not-knowing left unknown became origination, and because origination occurred a cessation is looked for; one who sees this has no separate origination-talk and no cessation-talk, freed from both, with no "he saw cessation," no "nibbāna was realized by him," no view-talk and no person-talk — no one who reached nibbāna and no one who goes, and the same for saṁsāra, no one who comes and no one who goes, for if saṁsāra itself is a distortion, who is there to come and who to go.
- **The dream simile: the un-locatable dog.** As in a dream there is no place, so a dog barking in a dream has no birthplace to be found, cannot be said to have come from a direction or gone in a direction, and no spot can be named where it is; seeing that very talk, that seeing is like nibbāna. Registering "a dog came and now is gone" is saṁsāra, and seeing that such a thing never happened — seeing the non-happening within the very talk — the "dream" is void, even "it is false" empties, and it can no longer be called a dream; for we call it a dream only because a dog appeared, stayed and ran off, and seeing that story just as it was formed it is no longer a dream — known to have been built as a distortion, and that very seeing is the cessation-view (*nirodha-dassana*).

## Summary

This second part holds that seeing the distortion as distortion is right view. The three — impermanent, suffering, not-self — are what the "permanent, pleasant, self" we have taken up really is, held so only from not knowing it a distortion; seeing that distortion as a distortion is itself the natural state, the clear right view. When the view is right, at the end nothing remained and nothing ceased — not a pleasure that became suffering, not a self that became not-self, not even a "dying," for "I existed and I die" comes only from "permanent-pleasant-self"; existence being built from a non-persistence, seeing the non-persistence as non-persistence there is no talk of existence or non-existence. Purifying this same view up to arahatship is nothing new — only the view sharpening until all views are seen to form, the wisdom settling so every arising word and consciousness is seen as it forms.

A formed thing can never yield origination. Asked what water is, one says H2O; asked further, the molecules, the atoms, endlessly — so we think we found water's cause, yet H2O too needs a cause, and hunting it a fresh cause appears while the old becomes a fruit, so looking back there is only fruit upon fruit; from a formed thing origination is never seen, which is the depth of the Buddha's word that cessation is realized only where origination is seen. We never find why the not-knowing is — the root of dependent-origination never falls to the mind — and until ignorance is seen as ignorance by ignorance itself, cessation is never realized, and just so long both a saṁsāra and an extinguishing "exist." Seeing thus, one is freed from both: no separate saṁsāra to be met, no separate nibbāna escaping it, the seeing of the distortedness of the formed "saṁsāra" being nibbāna — so there is no nibbāna apart from saṁsāra and none apart from nibbāna, and no extinguishing can be shown apart from origination. We suppose we have "attained" nibbāna, but speak of it only because of saṁsāra; the meeting-of-three having solidified a permanence, we hold "the existing thing was lost" and imagine a separate nibbāna free of it, not seeing that the very sense "there is something" arose from not-knowing — and seeing not-knowing by not-knowing, the root building both "it exists" and "it is lost" appears, both extremes seen. One who sees this has no separate origination- or cessation-talk, no "he saw cessation," no view-talk and no person-talk — no one who reaches nibbāna and none who goes, and the same for saṁsāra, for if saṁsāra itself is a distortion, who comes and who goes. As in a dream there is no place, a dog barking in a dream has no birthplace, no direction of coming or going, no spot where it is; registering "a dog came and now is gone" is saṁsāra, and seeing that such a thing never happened — the non-happening within the very talk — the dream is void, even "it is false" empties, and it can no longer be called a dream, known to have been built as a distortion, and that very seeing is the cessation-view.
