[English]
A discussion on the difference between ordinary anicca and the ariya understanding
that is accessible only through pañña, not viññana.
The teacher shows how taking an arisen thing and concluding "it didn't arise"
produces two separate saññā — not genuine insight.
True samudaya and nirodha must be seen simultaneously in one understanding,
like four facets of a single cetiya, not as sequential discoveries.
The catusacca is one indivisible insight, not four separate realisations.
