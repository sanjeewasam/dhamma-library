[English]
Part 1 of a discussion on jātiyam dukka (birth itself is
suffering) and udayavyaya ñāṇa. Through a silence exercise
and the 360-degree analogy, the teacher shows that hearing
capacity is always active — invariant whether sound is present
or absent. This nittya (permanent) foundation, built through
avijjā, is invisible: we never search for the floor we sit on.
Seeing this wrongly-constructed permanence as itself a
haṭagænīma (arising) is what "seeing anicca" means.
