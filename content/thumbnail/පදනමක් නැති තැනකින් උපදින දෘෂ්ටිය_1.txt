[English]
Part 1 of a discussion on how diṭṭhi (self-view) arises from
a groundless ground. The teacher reverses the usual sequence:
seeing produces the concepts of "eye" and "form," not the
other way round. Since "I" is born from seeing itself, the
base can never be seen — like feet cannot see the ground they
stand on. Hearing's base is shown to be a non-existence: neither
asti nor nāsti can be established there. Sakkāya is redefined
as arising within diṭṭha suta muta viññāta, not from a body.
