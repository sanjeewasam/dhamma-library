[English]
Part 1 of "Crossing over without establishing or striving."
Ven. Rēvata shows that in hētu-paccaya (conditionality),
nothing can be designated — no person, no thing, no result.
The milk-tea analogy: "tea" appears from ingredients yet
exists in none of them; that appearance is māyā. Avijjā is
the prior belief that something exists before encountering
it. Liberation requires no āyāsa — the fist opens and
nothing was ever held.

වලින් "තේ" පෙනී සිටියත් කිසිම අමුද්‍රව්‍යයක එය

"තිබේ" යැයි විශ්වාස කිරීමයි. මිදීමට ආයාසයක් අවශ්‍ය
