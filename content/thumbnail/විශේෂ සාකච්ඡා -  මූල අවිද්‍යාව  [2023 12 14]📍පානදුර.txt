[English]
A special discussion on mūla avijjā (root ignorance).
"Yamak yamek" — something/someone — is itself the root
delusion. The white-paper analogy shows pēnīma (seeing)
as a base that is never found yet never absent. Anicca
properly seen is not labelling things impermanent but
seeing that encounter itself has no start or end. Even
dhamma, if held as a fact, becomes diṭṭhi. Sammā diṭṭhi
is seeing from every angle that nothing can be grasped.
