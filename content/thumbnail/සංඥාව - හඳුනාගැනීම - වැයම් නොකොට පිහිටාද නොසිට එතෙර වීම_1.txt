Saññā — Recognition — Crossing Over Without Effort (Part 1)
The Buddha's mirage analogy for saññā points at non-existence,
but we make the mirage itself into "a thing." Recognition seems
to identify something external, yet the nimitta (sign) is entirely
internal — a child without it recognises nothing. Saying "recognition
never happened" falls to the nihilistic extreme; using dhamma
to reject experience makes dhamma itself into māra. Wisdom sees
delusion as delusion from within — without rejecting, without labelling.

ධර්මයම මාරයා වේ. ප්‍රඥාව මුලාව තුළ සිට මුලාව
මුලාව බවින්ම දකී — ප්‍රතික්ෂේප නොකොට, ලේබල් නොකොට.
